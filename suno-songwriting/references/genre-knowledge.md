# Genre Knowledge

Use genre labels as functional production directions, not as decoration. Prefer specific, compatible terms over long genre stacks.

For historical, regional, classical, folk, electronic, rock, pop, hip-hop, Latin, Afro-diasporic, devotional, and fusion genres, read `extended-genre-diversity-matrix.md` first. It routes to focused genre-family files with summaries, traits, and associated instruments.

## Common Prompt Dimensions

- **Genre**: pop, rock, country, folk, R&B, soul, funk, hip-hop, trap, EDM, house, techno, metal, punk, jazz, blues, reggae, gospel, ambient, cinematic, orchestral.
- **Era**: 60s girl group, 70s soul, 80s synth-pop, 90s alt-rock, 2000s pop punk, 2010s festival EDM, modern bedroom pop.
- **Scene/region**: Nashville country, Memphis soul, Chicago house, Detroit techno, West Coast hip-hop, UK garage, Afrobeat, K-pop, J-pop, bossa nova.
- **Energy**: intimate, restrained, driving, anthemic, club-ready, cinematic, aggressive, meditative.
- **Production**: lo-fi, polished, analog, glossy, live band, sample-based, acoustic, heavily sidechained, tape-saturated.

## Useful Pairings

- Pop + synth-pop: glossy hooks, gated drums, bright synth arps, clean vocal stacks.
- Country + folk: acoustic guitar, fiddle, mandolin, warm storytelling, restrained percussion.
- R&B + neo-soul: electric piano, syncopated drums, expressive runs, rich harmonies.
- Indie rock + shoegaze: jangly or washed guitars, roomy drums, hazy vocals.
- Hip-hop + trap: 808 bass, crisp hats, sparse melodic loop, confident cadence.
- EDM + house: four-on-the-floor kick, sidechained bass, build/drop structure.
- Metal + orchestral: palm-muted guitars, double kick, choir or strings, epic dynamics.
- Gospel + soul: organ, choir responses, handclaps, powerful lead vocal.

## Fusion Guardrails

- Combine at most 2-3 strong genre labels unless the user asks for experimentation.
- Make one genre the anchor and the others modifiers: "indie folk with subtle synth-pop textures" is clearer than "folk synth indie pop cinematic ambient".
- When referencing an artist, translate into traits: vocal range, groove, instrumentation, era, production texture, emotional tone.
- Avoid contradictory production cues unless contrast is intentional: "lo-fi intimate arena anthem" may confuse generation.
- For global or tradition-specific genres, preserve the identifying musical mechanism: rhythm cycle, ensemble role, vocal ornament, social function, or instrument palette.

## Genre Traits

- **Pop**: direct chorus, bright topline, polished drums, strong pre-chorus lift.
- **Rock**: guitars forward, live drum energy, dynamic chorus, riff or hook motif.
- **Country**: narrative lyric, acoustic rhythm guitar, pedal steel or fiddle, grounded vocal.
- **Folk**: organic instruments, vivid imagery, conversational melody, minimal production.
- **R&B/Soul**: groove-first, melisma, harmony stacks, bass and keys interplay.
- **Hip-hop/Rap**: rhythmic density, internal rhyme, beat pocket, ad-libs.
- **EDM**: build/drop arcs, synth leads, sidechain pulse, simple chantable hooks.
- **Jazz**: extended harmony, swung or brushed drums, improvisational feel.
- **Cinematic**: wide dynamics, orchestral percussion, strings/brass, trailer-like builds.

## Extended Genre Axes

- **Liturgical/classical**: chant, madrigal, fugue, waltz, minimalism, chamber-jazz hybrids.
- **Blues/jazz/roots**: Delta blues, bebop, bluegrass, zydeco, folk fiddle traditions.
- **Electronic/club**: house, techno, trance, neurofunk, witch house, Jersey club, hyperpop, future bass.
- **Heavy/rock**: math rock, post-rock, metalcore, symphonic power metal, pop punk, alternative rock.
- **Latin/Caribbean**: reggaeton, dembow, son cubano, bachata, tango nuevo, fado, flamenco.
- **African/diasporic**: Afrobeat, Afrobeats, amapiano, highlife, benga, gnawa, mbaqanga, kwaito.
- **South/East/Central Asian**: qawwali, dhrupad, gagaku, Noh hayashi, gamelan gong kebyar, Tuvan khoomei.
