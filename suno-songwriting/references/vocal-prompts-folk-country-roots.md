# Folk, Country, and Roots Vocal Prompts

VP019-VP024: story-forward diction, narrower ranges, vernacular inflection, and acoustic plausibility.

### VP019 — Cedar Thread Narrator
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Woody plainspoken folk alto with minimal ornament.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** folk alto with cedar-warm dry timbre, clear unadorned phonation, narrow chest-centered range, natural consonants, tiny scoops, and plainspoken storytelling. Acoustic singer-songwriter/folk arrangement with close room realism, guitar or piano support, and minimal processing. Verse 1 should carry the emotional center in low register, pre-chorus should rise gently, chorus should open modestly, bridge should sound completely exposed, breakdown should become a hand-played pulse or spoken refrain, final chorus should add simple harmony. Emphasize honesty and lyric intelligibility. Avoid fake rustic accent, over-singing, and glossy pop tuning.
- **Negative prompt / avoid list:** named folk artist, exaggerated rural dialect, heavy vibrato, big pop belt, overproduced sheen.
- **Suggested Suno tags:** [Front-Porch Intro], [Verse - Intimate Low Register], [Singalong Refrain], [Afterglow], [Resolved Cadence]
- **Expected audible result:** A grounded lyric-first reading with minimal vocal display.
- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP043 for cooler regional restraint and VP020 for high roots twang.

### VP020 — High Lonesome Kite
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Piercing high roots voice with forward twang and straight tone.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** high roots tenor/alto voice with piercing bright twang, forward nasal resonance, clear phonation, mostly straight tone, sharp consonants, open vowels, and rare controlled yodel-like breaks. Bluegrass/folk-pop arrangement with acoustic string-band energy and close harmony. Verse 1 should stay lean and clear, pre-chorus should climb with keening tension, chorus should ring high on open vowels, bridge should pull back to straight-tone vulnerability, breakdown should become a folk chant, final chorus should add harmony and tiny break ornaments. Emphasize high lonesome clarity. Avoid nasal caricature, overdone yodeling, and thin low notes.
- **Negative prompt / avoid list:** comic twang, excessive yodels, shrillness, fake accent.
- **Suggested Suno tags:** [Front-Porch Intro], [Singalong Refrain], [Harmony Stack], [Ad-Lib Finale], [Resolved Cadence]
- **Expected audible result:** A bright acoustic version that cuts through with straight-tone lift.
- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP024 for communal bell tenor and VP047 for alpine-clear soprano ornaments.

### VP021 — Porchlight Tremolo
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Warm country mezzo with soft tremolo shimmer.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** country mezzo voice with porchlight warmth, slightly nasal golden timbre, clear soft-edged phonation, relaxed vowel color, country scoops, and noticeable but controlled tremolo vibrato. Country-pop/Americana arrangement with acoustic guitar, gentle drums, and polished warmth. Verse 1 should tell the story plainly, pre-chorus should lift with tender scoops, chorus should open into a gentle belt, bridge should be vulnerable and close, breakdown should be rhythmic but friendly, final chorus should add harmony and tremolo shimmer. Emphasize warmth and resilience. Avoid exaggerated accent, wobbling out of tune, and overdone twang.
- **Negative prompt / avoid list:** parody twang, uncontrolled tremolo, muddy vowels, overproduced pop gloss.
- **Suggested Suno tags:** [Front-Porch Intro], [Turnaround Lick], [Big Chorus], [Harmony Stack], [Afterglow]
- **Expected audible result:** A tender country-pop interpretation with human tremolo color.
- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP023 for yodel breaks and VP045 for drier road-rock drive.

### VP022 — Dustbowl Story Baritone
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Dry weathered baritone narrator, spoken-sung and low.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** baritone narrator with dusty woody timbre, speech-like clear phonation, low chest-only range, rare rough vibrato, plain consonant-forward diction, and spoken-sung backphrasing. Americana/folk-blues arrangement with dry acoustic texture, minimal percussion, and tape-warm realism. Verse 1 should sound like testimony, pre-chorus should intensify through words not range, chorus should remain low and resolute, bridge should be bare and intimate, breakdown should be a spoken chant, final chorus should add rough group hums. Emphasize weathered witness and lyric clarity. Avoid fake rural caricature, pitchless mumbling, and over-polished production.
- **Negative prompt / avoid list:** named roots artist, exaggerated accent, too much gravel, melodic collapse, glossy pop mix.
- **Suggested Suno tags:** [Front-Porch Intro], [Spoken Intro], [Chanted Hook], [Group Response], [Afterglow]
- **Expected audible result:** A low, narrative version where the chorus feels like conviction rather than height.
- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP013 for polished baritone and VP007 for tenor rasp.

### VP023 — Riverbend Yodel Alto
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Bright roots alto with controlled yodel breaks and playful lifts.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** alto/mezzo roots voice with bright-warm tone, clear phonation, agile chest-head breaks, light quick vibrato, crisp diction, country scoops, and sparing yodel-like ornaments. Country-folk/bluegrass-pop arrangement with acoustic bounce and clean harmony support. Verse 1 should be clear and buoyant, pre-chorus should use small break ornaments, chorus should open with playful lift, bridge should soften without gimmicks, breakdown should keep a hand-clap folk pulse, final chorus should add controlled flips and harmony. Emphasize joy, agility, and restraint. Avoid comic yodeling, exaggerated dialect, and unstable breaks.
- **Negative prompt / avoid list:** parody twang, too many flips, pitch cracks, childish tone.
- **Suggested Suno tags:** [Second Verse Switch-Up], [Singalong Refrain], [Harmony Stack], [Ad-Lib Finale], [Resolved Cadence]
- **Expected audible result:** A roots-pop reading with audible but tasteful break ornaments.
- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP021 for tremolo warmth and VP055 for different laugh-cry ornament shape.

### VP024 — Appalachian Bell Tenor
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Ringing roots tenor optimized for harmony and singalong lift.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** roots tenor with ringing bell-bright timbre, open clear phonation, high chest/mix support, nearly straight tone, clear acoustic diction, and group-harmony-friendly phrase endings. Bluegrass/folk-rock arrangement with acoustic strings, offbeat chop, and stacked natural harmonies. Verse 1 should be earnest and direct, pre-chorus should rise cleanly, chorus should become a singalong with open vowels, bridge should reduce to exposed lead, breakdown should feel communal, final chorus should add harmony stacks. Emphasize bell tone and ensemble blend. Avoid exaggerated regional accent, shrill nasal tone, and overprocessed vocals.
- **Negative prompt / avoid list:** fake Appalachian caricature, harsh highs, robotic tuning, buried harmonies.
- **Suggested Suno tags:** [Front-Porch Intro], [Singalong Refrain], [Harmony Stack], [Group Response], [Resolved Cadence]
- **Expected audible result:** A bright acoustic singalong with strong harmony identity.
- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP020 for more piercing solo twang and VP036 for highlife groove warmth.
