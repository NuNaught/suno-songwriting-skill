# Vocal Delivery Tags

Vocal movement and performance cues for delivery changes inside lyrics. When used in lyric output, embed these cues inside the relevant section tag, such as `[Intro - Spoken Intro]`, `[Post-Chorus - Chanted Hook]`, or `[Final Chorus - Group Response]`; do not write them as standalone tags.

## Vocal and delivery behavior
- [Spoken Intro] — Opens with speech rather than full melodic singing. Expected result: immediacy, narrative intimacy, or scene-setting realism. Consider using when you want to establish voice, character, attitude, or documentary tone.

- [Chanted Hook] — Uses rhythm-forward repeated words or short phrases instead of fully melodic writing. Expected result: strong crowd recall, physicality, and directness. Consider using in club music, protest forms, sports-chant energy, ritual sections, or post-choruses.

- [Melismatic Lift] — Extends syllables across multiple notes to increase expressive charge. Expected result: more emotional intensity, vocal flourish, and genre-coded richness. Consider using in R&B, qawwali, gospel-influenced moments, flamenco-adjacent writing, or climactic lines.

- [Ecstatic Repetition] — Repeats a phrase insistently until it becomes trance-like or emotionally transcendent. Expected result: heightened immersion, spiritual charge, or cathartic insistence. Consider using when the goal is devotion, collective release, mantra power, or climactic obsession.

- [Group Response] — Brings in ensemble voices answering or reinforcing the lead. Expected result: enlarged social scale and stronger communal feeling. Consider using in call-and-response genres, anthem sections, carnival forms, folk refrains, and devotional peaks.

- [Lead Improvisation] — Gives the principal voice freedom to elaborate, ornament, or ad-lib beyond the fixed line. Expected result: spontaneity, virtuosity, and emotional specificity. Consider using when the style values expressive interpretation or live-feeling individuality.

- [Harmony Stack] — Densely layered harmonies reinforce a focal phrase or line. Expected result: weight, sheen, and emotional bloom. Consider using for hook emphasis, final choruses, dream-pop width, choral lift, or cinematic expansion.
