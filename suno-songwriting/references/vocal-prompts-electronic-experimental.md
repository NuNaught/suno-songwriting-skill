# Electronic and Experimental Vocal Prompts

VP025-VP030: production-shaped voices, repetition, drones, processing, and electronic instability.

### VP025 — Pixel Lace Soprano
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Crystalline glitch-pop soprano with micro-stutters.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** soprano electronic voice with pixel-bright crystalline timbre, clean airy phonation, straight tone, crisp micro-consonants, tiny glitch repeats, and quantized syncopation. Hyperpop/glitch-pop arrangement with bright synths, controlled tuning artifacts, chopped doubles, and clean sub energy. Verse 1 should feel fragile and close, pre-chorus should stutter upward, chorus should sparkle on open vowels, bridge should strip to dry human voice, breakdown should chop the chant while keeping words clear, final chorus should bloom in digital harmony. Emphasize crystalline detail and intelligibility. Avoid harsh highs, chaotic glitching, lyric destruction, and .
- **Negative prompt / avoid list:** unintelligible chops, painful treble, robotic monotone, unstable form.
- **Suggested Suno tags:** [Glitch Layer], [Hyperpop Glitch Refrain], [Big Chorus], [Hard Cut], [Ad-Lib Finale]
- **Expected audible result:** A fragile but electric version with digital articulation as identity.
- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP028 for fragmented choir behavior and VP057 for acoustic-metallic precision.

### VP026 — Vapor Monotone Oracle
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Detached spoken-sung low voice with vaporwave distance.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** low alto/baritone spoken-sung voice with cool matte timbre, soft dry speech phonation, narrow pitch movement, no vibrato, even articulation, and detached grid-aligned phrasing. Minimal synth/vaporwave-darkwave arrangement with filtered texture, tape warble, slow drums, and wide reverb. Verse 1 should feel like a distant confession, pre-chorus should rise only slightly, chorus should stay restrained and hypnotic, bridge should become eerie and exposed, breakdown should be spoken chant, final chorus should layer doubles without emotional melodrama. Emphasize controlled monotone and atmosphere. Avoid pitchless laziness, excessive reverb blur, and generic robotic voice.
- **Negative prompt / avoid list:** lazy monotone, inaudible words, random autotune, overdramatic belt.
- **Suggested Suno tags:** [Filtered Texture], [Loop Expansion], [Chanted Hook], [Process Reveal], [Unresolved Ending]
- **Expected audible result:** A cool, experimental reading where detachment becomes the main expressive lens.
- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP030 for deeper drone resonance and VP059 for warmer process minimalism.

### VP027 — Subpulse Chant Leader
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Low modal chant leader over sub-bass and sparse pulse.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** chant leader with dark grounded timbre, firm clear phonation, chest-dominant low register, no vibrato, percussive consonants, modal slides, and locked half-time groove. Ritual electronic/ambient bass arrangement with sub-bass, drone bed, sparse percussion, and low group responses. Verse 1 should feel ceremonial and restrained, pre-chorus should accumulate pressure, chorus should stay grounded and open, bridge should drop to drone and voice, breakdown should become the central chant, final chorus should close like a ritual with response voices. Emphasize low authority and repetition. Avoid monotony, muddied lows, fake sacred language, and excessive bass masking the vocal.
- **Negative prompt / avoid list:** parody ritual, buried vocal under sub, pitch drift, random growls.
- **Suggested Suno tags:** [Drone Bed], [Chant Over Sub Bass], [Half-Time Shift], [Chanted Hook], [Ritual Close]
- **Expected audible result:** A grounded low-chant version that tests repetition and sub-bass compatibility.
- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP041 for devotional bass austerity and VP030 for ambient low resonance.

### VP028 — Glitch Choir Sprite
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Playful fragmented digital choir lead with clipped stutters.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** processed choir lead with bright candy timbre, clean airy phonation, straight tone, clipped stutters, chopped harmony pings, and playful syncopation. Glitch-pop/hyperpop arrangement with tiny vocal fragments, hard cuts, harmony stacks, and elastic synth percussion. Verse 1 should begin as a single intimate lead, pre-chorus should multiply in fragments, chorus should bloom into mini-choir layers, bridge should return to exposed human voice, breakdown should chop the chant rhythmically, final chorus should reassemble into a bright digital choir. Emphasize playful uncanny identity while preserving the lyric. Avoid chaos, unintelligible chopping, and excessive tuning smear.
- **Negative prompt / avoid list:** destroyed lyrics, random glitches, harsh treble, unstable song form.
- **Suggested Suno tags:** [Glitch Layer], [Harmony Stack], [Hard Cut], [Hyperpop Glitch Refrain], [Return Bigger]
- **Expected audible result:** A high-risk digital ensemble reading where processing is structural.
- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP025 for solo crystalline focus and VP029 for smoother vocoder veil.

### VP029 — Frosted Vocoder Alto
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Cool alto with human-synthetic vocoder veil.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** alto voice with frosted smooth timbre, clear human phrasing under a restrained vocoder layer, flattened subtle vibrato, softened diction, and locked electronic groove. Synth-pop/electro-R&B arrangement with sidechain pads, glossy bass, and vocoder harmonies. Verse 1 should be filtered and intimate, pre-chorus should open the filter, chorus should widen into synthetic-human harmonies, bridge should reveal the dry human voice, breakdown should pulse with robotic portamento, final chorus should blend human ad-libs with vocoder stack. Emphasize human emotion inside processing. Avoid fully robotic lead, unintelligible words, and over-tuned sameness.
- **Negative prompt / avoid list:** full robot voice, vocoder masking lyrics, harsh autotune, generic EDM drop.
- **Suggested Suno tags:** [Filtered Texture], [Drone Into Club Beat], [Harmony Stack], [Main Drop], [Afterglow]
- **Expected audible result:** A cool electronic comparison where synthetic harmony color differentiates the profile.
- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP003 for falsetto-human agility and VP028 for fragmented digital choir.

### VP030 — Nocturne Drone Bass
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Cavernous bass-baritone with slow nocturnal drone resonance.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** bass-baritone voice with cavernous dark timbre, clear low resonance, slight creak, slow deliberate articulation, minimal vibrato, and drone-like sustains. Dark ambient/ritual folk arrangement with low drone bed, sparse percussion, and cavern reverb. Verse 1 should sound subterranean and intimate, pre-chorus should build pressure slowly, chorus should remain solemn and low, bridge should be almost unaccompanied, breakdown should become a deep chant, final chorus should widen with low harmonies rather than high belt. Emphasize low resonance and ceremonial gravity. Avoid muddy sub buildup, pitch smear, and comic bass exaggeration.
- **Negative prompt / avoid list:** novelty low voice, buried consonants, excessive cavern reverb, forced high notes.
- **Suggested Suno tags:** [Drone Bed], [Free Time], [Chanted Hook], [Ritual Close], [Unresolved Ending]
- **Expected audible result:** A dark, low-register version that stresses resonance and intelligibility.
- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP027 for more rhythmic chant and VP041 for austere devotional bass.
