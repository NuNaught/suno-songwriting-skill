# Diction-Forward Regional Color Vocal Prompts

VP043-VP048: broad or subtle vowel and consonant profiles used respectfully as musical variables.

### VP043 — North Sea Plainspoken Alto
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Cool plainspoken alto with subtle northern-coastal diction.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** alto narrator with cool grey timbre, dry clear phonation, minimal vibrato, clipped natural articulation, subtle northern-coastal English vowel color, and restrained speech-led phrasing. Indie folk/post-punk ballad arrangement with sparse guitar or piano, dry room, and understated rhythm. Verse 1 should be extremely clear and unromantic, pre-chorus should warm slightly, chorus should remain modest but firm, bridge should expose stoic tenderness, breakdown should be spoken-sung and precise, final chorus should add restrained harmony. Emphasize understatement and diction. Avoid caricature accent, flat lifelessness, and glossy pop polish.
- **Negative prompt / avoid list:** named indie singer, exaggerated regional accent, monotone boredom, overproduced mix, heavy vibrato.
- **Suggested Suno tags:** [Measured Silence], [Quiet Contrast], [Chanted Hook], [Afterglow], [Resolved Cadence]
- **Expected audible result:** A restrained indie version where subtle vowel shape and emotional economy matter.
- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP019 for warmer folk plainness and VP026 for artificial detachment.

### VP044 — Urban Pacific Cool Tenor
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Laid-back modern tenor with airy falsetto accents.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** tenor pop/R&B voice with cool smooth timbre, clear breathy-edged phonation, soft consonants, rounded vowels, light delayed vibrato, small R&B slides, and subtle modern Pacific-coast diction color. Alt-R&B/chill pop arrangement with minimal beat, glossy low end, and close-mic intimacy. Verse 1 should be low and relaxed, pre-chorus should lift through smooth mix, chorus should open without forcing, bridge should use soft falsetto, breakdown should stay laid-back and rhythmic, final chorus should add understated ad-libs. Emphasize cool restraint and pocket. Avoid generic breathy pop, exaggerated accent, and overprocessed tuning.
- **Negative prompt / avoid list:** mumble delivery, too much autotune, lifeless coolness, fake accent.
- **Suggested Suno tags:** [Locked Groove], [Filtered Texture], [Melismatic Lift], [Ad-Lib Finale], [Afterglow]
- **Expected audible result:** A modern chill interpretation where understatement and timing define the lead.
- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP003 for more kinetic falsetto and VP029 for synthetic processed coolness.

### VP047 — Alpine Clearbell Soprano
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Pure high clearbell soprano with tiny break ornaments.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** soprano folk-classical voice with alpine clearbell timbre, clean focused phonation, pure head-mix, straight tone with tiny shimmer, precise vowels, and rare controlled break-like ornaments. Chamber folk/classical-pop arrangement with acoustic space, light strings, and bell-like textures. Verse 1 should be clear and fresh, pre-chorus should climb in clean arcs, chorus should ring high and open, bridge should be pure and nearly still, breakdown should keep a delicate folk pulse, final chorus should add tiny break ornaments and high harmonies. ### VP048 — Coastal Iberian Lament Tenor
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Vowel-rich lament tenor with dignified rubato ache.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** tenor lament voice with salt-warm focused timbre, clear emotional phonation, chest-mix ache, delayed medium-wide vibrato, vowel-rich diction, sighing slides, and rubato phrasing. Fado-informed/Latin ballad chamber arrangement with acoustic guitar color, restrained percussion, and intimate room. Verse 1 should begin rubato and inward, pre-chorus should rise like a sigh, chorus should open into dignified lament, bridge should pause in fragile quiet, breakdown should pulse softly, final chorus should add aching ad-libs without over-singing. Emphasize longing and vowel color. Avoid imitating real fado or Latin singers, caricature accent, melodrama, and excessive vibrato.
- **Negative prompt / avoid list:** named singer, fake accent, overdramatic sobbing, too much reverb, blurred consonants.
- **Suggested Suno tags:** [Saudade Pause], [Tearful Rubato], [Big Chorus], [Lead Improvisation], [Afterglow]
- **Expected audible result:** A chamber-lament version where timing and vowels carry the emotion.
- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP056 for sharper tango drama and VP042 for formal heroic tenor.
