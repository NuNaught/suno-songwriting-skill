# Rock, Theater, and Metal Force Vocal Prompts

VP007-VP012: projection, grit, distortion, belt, stage-scale dynamics, and heavy vocal force.

### VP007 — Gravel Lantern Tenor
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Warm raspy roots-rock tenor with blue-note bends.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** tenor voice with warm gravel timbre, controlled rasp over a clear core, blue-note bends, irregular delayed vibrato, and behind-the-beat roots phrasing. Blues-rock/roots-rock arrangement with tape-warm drums, guitar grit, and live-room vocal presence. Verse 1 should smolder low and close, pre-chorus should lean into grit, chorus should bloom with raspy open vowels, bridge should reveal the clean core, breakdown should be half-spoken, final chorus should add raw but controlled ad-libs. Emphasize human wear and emotional resolve. Avoid uncontrolled distortion, pitch collapse, muddy lyrics, and .
- **Negative prompt / avoid list:** harsh screaming, unintelligible rasp, overpolished pop, fake accent.
- **Suggested Suno tags:** [Turnaround Lick], [Full Band Entry], [Big Chorus], [Breakdown], [Ad-Lib Finale]
- **Expected audible result:** A wounded but warm rock interpretation with grit intensifying over time.
- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP053 for rawer blues shouting and VP012 for lower, heavier roar.

### VP008 — Iron Bloom Mezzo
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Heavy mezzo with iron attack and blooming rock sustain.

Cinematic alternative-rock arrangement with wide drums, thick guitars, and controlled vocal saturation. Verse 1 should be restrained and tense, pre-chorus should press upward, chorus should bloom into strong sustained vowels, bridge should drop to exposed softness, final chorus should surge with dramatic harmonies. Emphasize defiant grief and controlled power. Avoid constant loudness, harsh clipping, over-theater diction, and .
- **Negative prompt / avoid list:** shouting all sections, muddy guitars covering vocal, strained upper notes.
- **Suggested Suno tags:** [Patient Build], [Wall-of-Guitars], [Big Chorus], [Return Bigger], [Final Surge]
- **Expected audible result:** A large rock-ballad voice with clear contrasts between restraint and peak.
- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP004 for pop control and VP050 for lower doom gravity.

### VP009 — Static Shout Baritone
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Dry clipped punk baritone, urgent and nearly vibrato-less.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** baritone voice with grainy dry timbre, speech-shout phonation, clipped consonants, chest-only delivery, no vibrato, and urgent ahead-of-beat phrasing. Raw post-punk/garage-rock arrangement with tight drums, angular guitars, and dry upfront vocal. Verse 1 should feel spoken and tense, pre-chorus should intensify through rhythm not range, chorus should be shouted-sung but pitched, bridge should briefly expose dry speech, breakdown should become a chant, final chorus should add gang backing. Emphasize directness and consonant attack. Avoid melodic over-singing, uncontrolled yelling, and excessive reverb.
- **Negative prompt / avoid list:** operatic vibrato, sloppy shouting, muddy diction, overproduced polish.
- **Suggested Suno tags:** [Guitar Count-In], [Locked Groove], [Chanted Hook], [Singalong Refrain], [Cold Ending]
- **Expected audible result:** A tense punk reading where rhythm and articulation define the character.
- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP051 for higher nasal punk and VP054 for industrial machine coldness.

### VP010 — Torchline Theater Belt
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Theatrical torch belter with crisp stage diction.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** theater-pop belter with ringing bright timbre, projected clear phonation, frontal resonance, stage-clear diction, dramatic scoops, and wide phrase-ending vibrato. Cinematic piano/orchestral-pop arrangement with spotlight vocal presence. Verse 1 should feel like an intimate monologue, pre-chorus should build dramatically, chorus should open into a torch-song belt, bridge should be exposed and acted quietly, breakdown should sharpen speech rhythm, final chorus should deliver large controlled ad-libs. Emphasize storytelling and dynamic contrast. Avoid parody, excessive vibrato, and constant belting.
- **Negative prompt / avoid list:** named theater performer, overacted diction, too much vibrato, shouting, generic Broadway parody.
- **Suggested Suno tags:** [Rubato Entrance], [Word-Painting Lift], [Big Chorus], [Quiet Contrast], [Ad-Lib Finale]
- **Expected audible result:** A narrative, stage-scale interpretation with obvious acting choices.
- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP004 for less theatrical pop belt and VP042 for classical-crossover support.

### VP011 — Cathedral Edge Countertenor
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Cool head-voice countertenor with ceremonial shimmer.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** countertenor-like voice with pale metallic timbre, clean head-voice phonation, straight-tone choral shimmer, precise legato diction, and ceremonial restraint. Sacred art-pop/classical crossover arrangement with cathedral ambience, drone undertone, and luminous chamber textures. Verse 1 should float quietly above the low arrangement, pre-chorus should rise like invocation, chorus should remain open but restrained, bridge should be ethereal and exposed, final chorus should add high harmony layers. Emphasize purity, distance, and controlled breath. Avoid operatic caricature, muddy reverb, and weak consonants.
- **Negative prompt / avoid list:** parody church sound, swallowed diction, excessive vibrato, shrill highs.
- **Suggested Suno tags:** [Drone Bed], [Rubato Entrance], [Prayerful Peak], [Layered Harmonies], [Afterglow]
- **Expected audible result:** A haunting high-register sacred-pop reading with strong spatial identity.
- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP038 for mezzo chamber purity and VP037 for low devotional stillness.

### VP012 — Smokehouse Roar
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Thick smoky rock baritone with controlled growl.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** baritone rock voice with smoky thick timbre, controlled growl on emphasized words, low chest dominance, rough wide vibrato, and heavy backbeat phrasing. Hard rock/blues-rock arrangement with warm distortion, room drums, and thick guitar support. Verse 1 should smolder in low register, pre-chorus should gather grit, chorus should roar on open vowels without losing pitch, bridge should pull down to smoky near-speech, breakdown should chant low, final chorus should be massive and weathered. Emphasize low power and stormy endurance. Avoid unintelligible growling, forced high tenor notes, and muddy mix.
- **Negative prompt / avoid list:** full scream throughout, buried lyrics, fake southern caricature, excessive distortion.
- **Suggested Suno tags:** [Full Band Entry], [Big Chorus], [Half-Time Shift], [Breakdown], [Final Surge]
- **Expected audible result:** A heavy low rock interpretation with growl used as punctuation.
- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP007 for warmer tenor grit and VP054 for colder industrial force.
