# Studio Pop and R&B Colorist Vocal Profiles

VP001-VP006: polished pop/R&B colorists differentiated by breath, melisma, brightness, register behavior, and syncopation.

#### VP001 — Glassline Soprano
- **Human-readable summary:** A bright, clean, high-set adult soprano lead with glassy tone, precise diction, and controlled delayed vibrato; built for testing clarity, high chorus lift, and synthetic-pop transparency.
- **Voice coordinate vector:** vocal_role=soprano lead; approximate_range=A3–F5; tessitura=high-set; vocal_weight=light lyric; timbre=glassy, bright, clear; phonation=clean with slight breath at phrase starts; register_behavior=head-mix dominant, seamless upper lift; vibrato=delayed narrow vibrato; ornamentation=small upward slides, restrained turns; articulation=crisp consonants, vowel-forward choruses; diction/accent=neutral studio, hidden accent color; rhythmic_feel=straight with slight forward momentum; genre_gravity=contemporary pop, synth-pop, art-pop; emotional_stance=hopeful precision; dynamic_shape=controlled crescendo into chorus; production_texture=glossy close-mic with light doubling; movement_behavior=low verse restraint, bright open chorus, polished final ad-libs.
- **Distinguishing features:** High clarity, clean tone, delayed vibrato, polished vowel alignment.
- **Likely strengths:** Lyric intelligibility, open vowels, layered harmonies, stable chorus peaks.
- **Likely weaknesses/failure modes:** May become generic pop if prompt lacks timbral specificity; excessive tuning can erase human nuance.
- **Best genres:** Synth-pop, commercial pop, cinematic electronic ballad, chamber-pop.
- **Contrasting nearest-neighbor profiles:** VP005 Chrome Melisma Weaver; VP040 Madrigal Silver Soprano.
- **Suno-relevant style vocabulary:** glassy soprano, clean phonation, delayed vibrato, precise diction, glossy pop, high-set chorus, light harmony stack.
- **What to listen for:** Whether the final chorus stays brilliant and clear without becoming shrill or anonymous.

#### VP002 — Velvet Alto Courier
- **Human-readable summary:** A warm, mid-low adult alto with velvet-dark tone, speech-like verses, and quietly authoritative delivery; designed for confessional intimacy and diction detail.
- **Voice coordinate vector:** vocal_role=alto lead; approximate_range=F3–D5; tessitura=middle-low; vocal_weight=medium-light; timbre=velvet, dark, smooth; phonation=soft clear with breathy edges; register_behavior=chest-mix blend, avoids high strain; vibrato=slow delayed vibrato on long notes; ornamentation=small scoops and sighs; articulation=soft consonants but clear word endings; diction/accent=neutral conversational, subtle diction foregrounding; rhythmic_feel=slightly behind the beat; genre_gravity=singer-songwriter pop, neo-soul, adult contemporary; emotional_stance=confessional, tender; dynamic_shape=gradual expansion; production_texture=dry close-mic, warm compression; movement_behavior=strong low verse, intimate bridge, rounded chorus lift.
- **Distinguishing features:** Dark alto color, behind-the-beat phrasing, calm authority.
- **Likely strengths:** Verses, bridges, emotional nuance, low-register intelligibility.
- **Likely weaknesses/failure modes:** Chorus may under-project unless open-throat lift is requested; breathiness can blur diction.
- **Best genres:** Neo-soul ballad, folk-pop, adult contemporary, intimate R&B.
- **Contrasting nearest-neighbor profiles:** VP006 Duskmilk Contralto Whisper; VP016 Late-Night Cassia Alto.
- **Suno-relevant style vocabulary:** velvet alto, close-mic warmth, soft chest mix, restrained vibrato, behind-the-beat phrasing.
- **What to listen for:** Whether the same lyric feels like private testimony rather than a conventional pop lead.

#### VP003 — Neon Falsetto Runner
- **Human-readable summary:** A nimble adult tenor/countertenor-adjacent voice centered on falsetto flashes, light rhythmic agility, and neon electronic sheen.
- **Voice coordinate vector:** vocal_role=tenor high lead; approximate_range=C3–G5; tessitura=upper-mid to high; vocal_weight=featherweight; timbre=bright, airy, neon; phonation=breathy falsetto and clear light mix; register_behavior=frequent chest-to-falsetto flips; vibrato=mostly straight tone, occasional shimmer; ornamentation=quick flips, tiny yelps, upward scoops; articulation=light and agile; diction/accent=neutral pop, hidden accent; rhythmic_feel=syncopated, agile, slightly ahead; genre_gravity=electropop, future bass, dance-pop; emotional_stance=euphoric nervous energy; dynamic_shape=quick lift into hooks; production_texture=glossy, doubled, lightly pitch-colored but human; movement_behavior=verse restraint, falsetto pre-chorus lift, final ad-lib flashes.
- **Distinguishing features:** Falsetto-forward register behavior and fast rhythmic delivery.
- **Likely strengths:** Hooks, post-chorus figures, high ad-libs, electronic textures.
- **Likely weaknesses/failure modes:** Can sound thin in quiet bridge; pitch correction may over-sanitize register breaks.
- **Best genres:** Future bass pop, electropop, dance-pop, hyper-clean R&B-pop.
- **Contrasting nearest-neighbor profiles:** VP001 Glassline Soprano; VP029 Frosted Vocoder Alto.
- **Suno-relevant style vocabulary:** airy falsetto runner, neon tone, register flips, syncopated topline, glossy electronic pop.
- **What to listen for:** Whether falsetto flips are audible as identity, not random instability.

#### VP004 — Amber Belt Architect
- **Human-readable summary:** A medium-heavy adult mezzo with amber brightness, open-throat belt, clear architecture across sections, and radio-friendly strength.
- **Voice coordinate vector:** vocal_role=mezzo belter; approximate_range=G3–E5; tessitura=middle-high; vocal_weight=medium-heavy; timbre=amber, bright-warm, ringing; phonation=clear, energized, slightly pressed at peaks; register_behavior=mix-belt dominant; vibrato=medium delayed vibrato after straight-tone attacks; ornamentation=clean scoops, occasional bluesy fall; articulation=clear theatrical-pop diction; diction/accent=neutral studio, subtle foregrounding; rhythmic_feel=straight, commanding; genre_gravity=pop rock, adult contemporary, theater-pop; emotional_stance=triumphant but grounded; dynamic_shape=terraced growth, strong final surge; production_texture=polished band-pop with layered chorus vocals; movement_behavior=low verse controlled, pre-chorus rising, chorus belted on open vowels.
- **Distinguishing features:** Strong controlled belt without rasp; emotionally direct.
- **Likely strengths:** Choruses, final climaxes, sustained open vowels.
- **Likely weaknesses/failure modes:** May overpower intimate sections; can drift into generic theatrical belting.
- **Best genres:** Pop rock, power ballad, adult contemporary, theater-pop crossover.
- **Contrasting nearest-neighbor profiles:** VP010 Torchline Theater Belt; VP052 Symphonic Steel Soprano.
- **Suno-relevant style vocabulary:** amber mezzo belt, open-throat chorus, clear mix, delayed vibrato, polished pop-rock lift.
- **What to listen for:** Whether the belt arrives as earned expansion rather than constant loudness.

#### VP005 — Chrome Melisma Weaver
- **Human-readable summary:** A sleek adult R&B tenor/alto-range lead with chrome-bright tone, elastic melisma, and liquid syncopation; ideal for testing ornament density under shared lyrics.
- **Voice coordinate vector:** vocal_role=high tenor or alto lead; approximate_range=B2–E5; tessitura=middle-high; vocal_weight=light-medium; timbre=chrome, smooth, glossy; phonation=clear with breathy starts; register_behavior=fluid mix and head voice; vibrato=fast narrow ornamental vibrato; ornamentation=melisma, slides, turnarounds, grace-note falls; articulation=liquid vowels, softened consonants; diction/accent=neutral R&B studio, subtle speech rhythm; rhythmic_feel=behind-the-beat syncopation; genre_gravity=contemporary R&B, neo-soul, quiet storm pop; emotional_stance=sensual, searching; dynamic_shape=ornamental growth; production_texture=smooth close-mic, stacked harmonies; movement_behavior=melismatic pre-chorus, ornamented chorus tails, ad-lib finale.
- **Distinguishing features:** Melisma as primary identity with polished tone.
- **Likely strengths:** Pre-chorus lift, bridge embellishment, final ad-libs.
- **Likely weaknesses/failure modes:** Can over-ornament and obscure lyric clarity; may ignore rhythmic second verse.
- **Best genres:** Contemporary R&B, neo-soul, pop-soul, slow jam.
- **Contrasting nearest-neighbor profiles:** VP001 Glassline Soprano; VP014 Honeyed Gospel Anchor.
- **Suno-relevant style vocabulary:** chrome R&B lead, liquid melisma, narrow vibrato, behind-the-beat phrasing, smooth stacked harmonies.
- **What to listen for:** Whether melisma lands on intentional emotional words rather than every syllable.

#### VP006 — Duskmilk Contralto Whisper
- **Human-readable summary:** A very low, breath-veiled adult contralto profile with intimate delivery, dark timbre, and sparse ornament; useful for testing whether the song can survive low, quiet interpretation.
- **Voice coordinate vector:** vocal_role=contralto lead; approximate_range=E3–B4; tessitura=low-set; vocal_weight=medium but softly delivered; timbre=dark, creamy, dusky; phonation=breathy, close-mic, low airflow; register_behavior=chest-dominant, minimal upper lift; vibrato=almost none, slight late tremble; ornamentation=descending sighs, micro-scoops; articulation=slow, soft-edged, intimate; diction/accent=neutral with hidden accent, very subtle; rhythmic_feel=laid-back, floating; genre_gravity=ambient pop, indie ballad, smoky lounge; emotional_stance=haunted tenderness; dynamic_shape=small dynamic window, quiet final bloom; production_texture=dry vocal with room tone, minimal reverb; movement_behavior=low verse emphasized, bridge exposed, chorus widened by harmonies instead of volume.
- **Distinguishing features:** Low tessitura, breath veil, nearly vibrato-less intimacy.
- **Likely strengths:** Quiet bridge, intro, confessional verse.
- **Likely weaknesses/failure modes:** Choruses may lack energy; intelligibility can suffer if breath too heavy.
- **Best genres:** Ambient pop, slow indie, noir lounge, minimal electronic ballad.
- **Contrasting nearest-neighbor profiles:** VP002 Velvet Alto Courier; VP041 Dhrupad Stone Bass.
- **Suno-relevant style vocabulary:** dusky contralto, breath-veiled close mic, low chest register, minimal vibrato, haunted restraint.
- **What to listen for:** Whether the chorus becomes emotionally larger without forcing high notes.
