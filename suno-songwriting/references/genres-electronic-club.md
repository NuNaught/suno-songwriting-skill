# Electronic and Club Genres

Use for electronic production, club forms, internet-pop extremes, dance drops, and beat-based atmospheres.

| Genre name | Summary | Key identifying characteristics | Associated instruments |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| House EDM                                  | Club-oriented electronic dance music descended from Chicago house.                      | Four-on-the-floor kick, loop-based grooves, offbeat hi-hats, repetitive build-and-release.                           | Drum machines, synthesizers, samplers, DAW                                  |
| Neurofunk                                  | Dark, highly engineered branch of drum and bass.                                        | Fast breakbeats, reese bass design, compressed texture, intricate sound design.                                      | Samplers, synths, DAW                                                       |
| Witch house                                | Murky, slow electronic style with occult/goth aesthetics.                               | Dragged tempos, chopped vocals, lo-fi haze, ominous synth textures.                                                  | Synthesizers, samplers, drum programming                                    |
| Jersey club                                | Fast East Coast club music built for dance battles and chopped samples.                 | Kick triplets, bed squeaks/chops, short loop structures, high-energy tempo.                                          | Sampler, drum programming, voice chops                                      |
| Hyperpop                                   | Maximalist internet-native pop exaggerating digital production and vocal treatment.     | Extreme pitch processing, clipped or blown-out textures, abrupt structure changes, candy-colored or chaotic timbres. | Voice, DAW, synths, samplers, drum programming                              |
| Future bass                                | Festival and streaming-era electronic pop/dance genre.                                  | Detuned synth chords, sidechain swell, emotional builds, bright melodic drops.                                       | Synthesizers, drum programming, samplers                                    |
| Dubstep (brostep/mainstream EDM variant)   | Aggressive festival-oriented electronic bass music.                                     | Half-time drops, modulated bass growls, high-impact builds, dramatic contrasts.                                      | Synthesizers, samplers, DAW                                                 |
| Techno                                     | Repetitive machine-driven electronic dance music rooted in hypnotic groove.             | Steady 4/4 pulse, minimal harmonic motion, timbral evolution, club-functional structure.                             | Drum machines, synths, sequencers                                           |
| Trance                                     | Melodic electronic dance genre built around uplift and extended build-release arcs.     | Arpeggiated synth lines, long risers, euphoric breakdowns, steady kick pulse.                                        | Synthesizers, drum machines, sequencers                                     |
| Lo-fi hip-hop                              | Relaxed beat-based style that became a major streaming and internet genre.              | Dusty samples, soft swing, jazzy loops, subdued textures, low-intensity groove.                                      | Sampler, drum programming, electric piano, vinyl-texture processing         |
| EDM festival pop                           | Pop songs built around large-scale electronic drop structure and crossover vocals.      | Verse-build-drop format, anthemic toplines, maximal impact mixing, broad commercial accessibility.                   | Voice, synths, drum machines, samplers                                      |
