# Edge and Extreme Hybrid Vocal Prompts

VP049-VP054: rasp, screams, doom, industrial tone, and high-force edge profiles.

### VP049 — Razor Choir Screamer
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Controlled harsh/clean heavy lead with gang chorus contrast.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** heavy vocalist with razor-bright controlled harsh phonation, clean mix-voice chorus contrast, percussive consonants, no vibrato in screams, brief vibrato in clean lines, and aggressive half-time rhythmic feel. Metalcore/post-hardcore arrangement with downtuned guitars, double-kick accents, gang shouts, and a heavy breakdown. Verse 1 should use intelligible harsh-spoken texture, pre-chorus should transition toward clean tension, chorus should be clean and huge, bridge should expose a fragile clean voice, breakdown should scream the chant clearly, final chorus should mix clean lead with harsh responses. Emphasize contrast and intelligibility. Avoid unsafe-sounding uncontrolled screaming, lyric destruction, and random growls.
- **Negative prompt / avoid list:** named metal vocalist, unintelligible screams, constant growling, pitchless clean chorus, overcompressed mush.
- **Suggested Suno tags:** [Double-Kick Launch], [Breakdown], [Half-Time Shift], [Gang Vocals], [Final Surge]
- **Expected audible result:** A high-risk heavy version where harsh/clean contrast reveals structure.
- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP051 for clean punk urgency and VP054 for industrial distortion.

### VP050 — Velvet Doom Contralto
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Low dark contralto with slow doom grandeur.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** contralto doom lead with black velvet timbre, clear low chest phonation, slight creak, slow wide vibrato, low slides, and heavy behind-the-beat phrasing. Doom metal/gothic darkwave arrangement with slow guitars, organ or synth drone, cavern ambience, and massive low drums. Verse 1 should sound like a low dirge, pre-chorus should swell slowly, chorus should expand darkly without high belt, bridge should be a near-whisper, breakdown should become a heavy low chant, final chorus should return massive with low harmonies. Emphasize majestic grief and low resonance. Avoid muddy words, tempo drag beyond usability, and forced high notes.
- **Negative prompt / avoid list:** named doom singer, novelty low voice, unintelligible lows, endless slow intro, overdistorted vocal.
- **Suggested Suno tags:** [Drone Bed], [Half-Time Shift], [Cloudburst], [Chanted Hook], [Ritual Close]
- **Expected audible result:** A dark, slow, massive interpretation with contralto gravity.
- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP006 for whisper-low intimacy and VP008 for faster rock mezzo force.

### VP051 — Neon Punk Nasal Lead
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Fast nasal pop-punk lead with clipped consonants.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** punk-pop lead with neon bright nasal timbre, pressed-clear phonation, chest-mix shout peaks, no vibrato, clipped consonants, and urgent ahead-of-beat delivery. Pop-punk/power-pop arrangement with fast guitars, punchy drums, dry lead vocal, and gang backups. Verse 1 should move quickly with precise diction, pre-chorus should accelerate emotionally, chorus should be a shouted singalong, bridge should briefly strip to raw voice, breakdown should become crowd chant, final chorus should explode with gang vocals. Emphasize speed, clarity, and restless defiance. Avoid childish parody, out-of-tune shouting, and over-slick pop polish.
- **Negative prompt / avoid list:** named pop-punk singer, fake teenage voice, sloppy pitch, excessive nasal caricature, buried lyrics.
- **Suggested Suno tags:** [Guitar Count-In], [Double-Time Lift], [Singalong Refrain], [Chanted Hook], [Cold Ending]
- **Expected audible result:** A bright, fast version where consonants and energy define the identity.
- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP009 for lower post-punk shout and VP060 for dance-festival crowd energy.

### VP052 — Symphonic Steel Soprano
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Dramatic steel soprano with heroic symphonic-metal lift.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** dramatic soprano voice with steel-bright ringing timbre, supported clear phonation, powerful high head/mix register, wide controlled vibrato, formal diction, and heroic sustained peaks. Symphonic metal/cinematic rock arrangement with orchestral layers, guitars, drums, and choir support. Verse 1 should be restrained and luminous, pre-chorus should ascend dramatically, chorus should reach an anthemic high peak, bridge should be prayerful and exposed, breakdown should add heavy rhythmic contrast, final chorus should deliver high ad-libs and choir-scale lift. Emphasize heroic transcendence and control. Avoid operatic parody, shrill highs, and burying words under orchestra.
- **Negative prompt / avoid list:** named metal soprano, excessive opera vibrato, piercing treble, muddy choir, constant fortissimo.
- **Suggested Suno tags:** [Grand Overture], [Double-Kick Launch], [Symphonic Choir Layer], [Anthemic Peak], [Final Surge]
- **Expected audible result:** A grand symphonic version with a clear high-register climax.
- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP004 for pop-rock belt and VP042 for heroic tenor support.

### VP053 — Rusted Blues Shouter
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Raw blues shouter with rusted timbre and bent notes.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** blues shouter with rusted copper-dark timbre, raspy speech-rooted phonation, chest-dominant delivery, irregular wide vibrato, blue-note bends, growled tails, moans, and loose backphrased swing. Raw blues/soul-rock arrangement with guitar grit, room drums, and live imperfections. Verse 1 should feel spoken from the gut, pre-chorus should bend upward, chorus should shout open vowels with rough pitch center, bridge should drop into a moaning quiet, breakdown should be stomp-like chant, final chorus should add raw ad-libs. Emphasize bent-note emotion and grit. Avoid fake blues caricature, unintelligible rasp, and overproduced polish.
- **Negative prompt / avoid list:** named blues singer, parody blues accent, pitch collapse, excessive growl, glossy pop compression.
- **Suggested Suno tags:** [Turnaround Lick], [Locked Groove], [Big Chorus], [Chanted Hook], [Lead Improvisation]
- **Expected audible result:** A raw version where blue-note inflection transforms the melodic contour.
- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP007 for warmer rock grit and VP045 for road-song twang.

### VP054 — Blackroom Industrial Baritone
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Cold industrial baritone with clipped machine diction.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** industrial baritone with cold metallic-dark timbre, pressed speech phonation, controlled distortion at phrase edges, chest-only delivery, no vibrato, hard clipped consonants, and mechanical grid-locked rhythm. Industrial rock/dark electronic arrangement with distorted drums, synth noise, half-time pulse, and filtered vocal doubles. Verse 1 should sound like a machine-room confession, pre-chorus should build through pressure, chorus should be shouted and dark, bridge should strip to dry spoken voice, breakdown should become pulse chant, final chorus should stack distorted doubles. Emphasize cold menace and precision. Avoid unintelligible distortion, cartoon villain tone, and overcompressed mush.
- **Negative prompt / avoid list:** named industrial singer, fake robot voice, buried lyrics, random distortion, excessive reverb.
- **Suggested Suno tags:** [Filtered Texture], [Half-Time Shift], [Chanted Hook], [Noise Wash], [Hard Cut]
- **Expected audible result:** A dark electronic-rock version where machine diction defines the vocal.
- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP009 for organic post-punk shout and VP049 for harsh heavy contrast.
