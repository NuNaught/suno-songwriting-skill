# Cross-Cultural Process Vocal Prompts

VP055-VP060: ornament, interlocking rhythm, minimalist pulse, chant, festival, and dance-social behavior.

### VP055 — Klezmer Laugh-Cry Tenor
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Reedy tenor with laugh-cry ornaments and bittersweet lift.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** tenor with bright reedy timbre, clear forward phonation, expressive cracks, fast ornamental vibrato, laugh-cry turns, sob-like falls, and flexible rubato into dance pulse. Klezmer-fusion/cabaret folk arrangement with clarinet or accordion color, acoustic rhythm, and lively ensemble responses. Verse 1 should begin as lament, pre-chorus should rise with ornamented turns, chorus should become bittersweet and dance-like, bridge should be tender and rubato, breakdown should become call-response folk chant, final chorus should lift with expressive ornaments. Emphasize bittersweet celebration. Avoid caricature accent, novelty ornaments, and obscured lyrics.
- **Negative prompt / avoid list:** named klezmer singer, parody dialect, too many ornaments, comedic tone, unclear words.
- **Suggested Suno tags:** [Tearful Rubato], [Wedding Peak], [Call and Response], [Lead Improvisation], [Resolved Cadence]
- **Expected audible result:** A bittersweet folk-cabaret version where ornament sounds like laughter and crying.
- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP048 for lament tenor and VP023 for roots break ornaments.

### VP056 — Tango Ash Alto
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Smoky tango alto with sharp pauses and urban-night ache.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** alto dramatic lead with ash-smoky focused timbre, clear emotional phonation, chest-mix upper pressure, delayed medium vibrato, precise consonants, sighing slides, sharp pauses, and rubato against tango pulse. Tango nuevo/cinematic cabaret arrangement with bandoneon-like color, piano, bass, and restrained percussion. Verse 1 should be dramatic and rubato, pre-chorus should tighten the pulse, chorus should be taut and aching, bridge should suspend in a saudade-like pause, breakdown should pulse with spoken intensity, final chorus should swell then cut cleanly. Emphasize tension, timing, and restraint. Avoid caricature accent, melodramatic sobbing, and overbusy arrangement.
- **Negative prompt / avoid list:** named tango singer, fake accent, parody drama, excessive vibrato, cluttered bandoneon.
- **Suggested Suno tags:** [Bandoneon Lament], [Tearful Rubato], [Saudade Pause], [Chanted Hook], [Cold Ending]
- **Expected audible result:** A tense cinematic version where pauses are as important as notes.
- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP032 for clave-driven alto and VP048 for coastal-lament tenor.

### VP057 — Gamelan Spark Soprano
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Bell-bright soprano with interlocking rhythmic precision.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** soprano rhythmic lead with sparkling bell-like timbre, clear focused phonation, head-mix agility, straight tone, precise articulation, quick turns, repeated cells, and interlocking rhythmic feel. ### VP058 — Braided Drone Tenor
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Airy modal folk tenor braided with drone and circular phrasing.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** modal folk tenor with airy reedy timbre, clear breath-halo phonation, chest-mix center, slow narrow vibrato, vowel-centered legato, modal slides, and circular free-to-pulse phrasing. Ambient folk/chamber drone-pop arrangement with drone bed, acoustic strings, and light hand percussion. Intro should establish a drone, Verse 1 should feel like a pilgrim song, pre-chorus should rise gradually, chorus should open in modal vowels, bridge should become still and exposed, breakdown should chant over the drone, final chorus should return as ritual-folk lift. Emphasize searching tone and coherent modal ornament. Avoid vague world-music clichés, fake accent, and wandering pitch.

- **Suggested Suno tags:** [Drone Bed], [Loop Expansion], [Chanted Hook], [Ritual Close], [Afterglow]

- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP037 for devotional baritone drone and VP047 for high clear folk-classical tone.

### VP059 — Minimalist Pulse Alto
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Matte clean alto with locked pulse and micro-variation.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** alto process voice with clear matte neutral-warm timbre, clean non-breathy phonation, narrow stable range, nearly straight tone, precise even diction, and locked pulse phrasing with micro-variation. Minimalist art-pop/techno-pop arrangement with ostinato bed, pulsing synths or mallets, gradual layering, and restrained reverb. Verse 1 should establish the pattern, pre-chorus should add small interval expansion, chorus should grow by layering not belting, bridge should expose a single repeated line softly, breakdown should become a strict chant, final chorus should return bigger through process accumulation. Emphasize repetition with subtle change. Avoid emotional flatness, robotic monotony, and overdecorated vocals.
- **Negative prompt / avoid list:** boring loop, robotic voice, excessive runs, static dynamics.
- **Suggested Suno tags:** [Ostinato Bed], [Process Reveal], [Loop Expansion], [Chanted Hook], [Return Bigger]
- **Expected audible result:** A hypnotic comparison where gradual accumulation is the primary vocal behavior.
- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP026 for detached monotone and VP057 for brighter interlocking process.

### VP060 — Carnival Circuit Lead
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Glossy festival-carnival dance lead with crowd callouts.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** festival dance lead with bright glossy timbre, energetic clear phonation, mix-belt peaks, brief pop-straight vibrato, percussive crowd-clear diction, callouts, and locked dancefloor syncopation. EDM festival-pop/global carnival arrangement with synth brass, big drums, group responses, riser builds, and bright percussion. Verse 1 should be intimate but beat-ready, pre-chorus should build like a riser, chorus should hit as dancefloor peak, bridge should strip to vocal and pad, breakdown should become crowd chant, final chorus should surge with call-and-response and ad-libs. Emphasize euphoria and structure. Avoid generic festival drops, crowd noise burying lyrics, and overhyped shouting.
- **Negative prompt / avoid list:** named EDM vocalist, generic sample-pack voice, fake accent, chaotic crowd, overcompressed drop.
- **Suggested Suno tags:** [Riser Build], [Main Drop], [Dancefloor Peak], [Group Response], [Final Surge]
- **Expected audible result:** A large-scale festival version that keeps the shared lyric audible through production size.
- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP031 for smoother club tenor and VP035 for parade-mezzo acoustic carnival scale.
