# Jazz, Blues, Country, and Roots Genres

Use for blues, jazz, folk-rooted storytelling, acoustic string-band, country, and adult songwriter lanes.

| Genre name | Summary | Key identifying characteristics | Associated instruments |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Delta blues                                | Early African American blues associated with the U.S. South.                            | Solo voice-plus-guitar texture, blue notes, slide guitar, flexible phrasing.                                         | Voice, acoustic guitar, harmonica                                           |
| Bebop                                      | Fast, harmonically dense modern jazz of the 1940s.                                      | Rapid tempos, asymmetrical melodies, advanced substitutions, virtuosic improvisation.                                | Alto/tenor sax, trumpet, piano, bass, drum kit                              |
| Bluegrass                                  | U.S. acoustic string-band music with virtuosic ensemble picking.                        | High lonesome vocals, rapid instrumental breaks, offbeat chop accompaniment.                                         | Banjo, mandolin, fiddle, acoustic guitar, upright bass                      |
| Zydeco                                     | Creole dance music from Louisiana.                                                      | Driving two-step grooves, accordion-led melodies, rubboard rhythm, party atmosphere.                                 | Accordion, washboard/rubboard, electric guitar, bass, drums                 |
| Hardanger fiddle tradition                 | Norwegian fiddle dance music with resonant drone-rich sound.                            | Sympathetic strings, asymmetrical dance rhythms, ornamented fiddle lines.                                            | Hardanger fiddle                                                            |
| Sevdalinka                                 | Bosnian urban song tradition noted for lyrical intimacy.                                | Ornamented melody, emotional rubato, poetic melancholy, flexible accompaniment.                                      | Voice, accordion, saz, violin                                               |
| Klezmer                                    | Ashkenazi Jewish instrumental wedding and celebration music.                            | Laughing/crying ornaments, modal inflection, dance sets, expressive lead lines.                                      | Clarinet, violin, tsimbl/cimbalom, accordion, brass                         |
| Contemporary country pop                   | Modern country fused with polished mainstream pop production.                           | Verse-chorus pop forms, country lyric themes, glossy vocal production, restrained twang markers.                     | Voice, acoustic guitar, electric guitar, bass, drums, banjo                 |
| Country rock / bro-country mainstream lane | Arena-scale commercial country drawing heavily from rock and pop.                       | Big choruses, loud drums, distorted guitars, country signifiers inside pop-rock structure.                           | Voice, electric guitar, acoustic guitar, bass, drum kit                     |
| Singer-songwriter pop                      | Mainstream or near-mainstream pop centered on personal vocal storytelling.              | Lyric focus, relatively sparse accompaniment, strong melodic identity, intimate delivery.                            | Voice, piano, acoustic guitar, light rhythm section                         |
| Adult contemporary                         | Smooth, broadly accessible mainstream style aimed at wide radio listenership.           | Moderate tempos, polished arrangements, emotional clarity, low rhythmic aggression.                                  | Voice, piano, guitars, soft synths, drums                                   |
