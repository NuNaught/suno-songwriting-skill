# Studio Pop and R&B Colorist Vocal Prompts

VP001-VP006: polished pop/R&B colorists differentiated by breath, melisma, brightness, register behavior, and syncopation.

### VP001 — Glassline Soprano
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Glassy high-set clean soprano with delayed narrow vibrato.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** soprano voice with glassy bright timbre, clean phonation, precise neutral diction, delayed narrow vibrato, and seamless head-mix lift. Glossy synth-pop/art-pop arrangement with light vocal doubles and crystalline pads. Verse 1 should feel intimate and low-lit, pre-chorus should rise cleanly, chorus should open into sustained bright vowels, bridge should be exposed and pure, final chorus should add polished ad-libs and harmony shimmer. Emphasize clarity, high-set lift, controlled vibrato. Avoid shrill highs, excessive pitch correction, and generic pop smoothing.
- **Negative prompt / avoid list:** shrill soprano, over-tuned robotic lead, buried lyrics, exaggerated vibrato.
- **Suggested Suno tags:** [Rubato Entrance], [Big Chorus], [Harmony Stack], [Ad-Lib Finale], [Afterglow]
- **Expected audible result:** A luminous, precise high lead whose chorus sounds open and polished without losing diction.
- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP040 for chamber purity and VP005 for ornament density.

Verse 1 should feel private and low-register, pre-chorus should gather breath, chorus should bloom rather than belt, bridge should feel whispered but clear, final chorus should add low harmonies. Emphasize warmth, restraint, emotional detail. Avoid overbreathiness, muddy consonants, generic lounge, and .

- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP006 for deeper quietness and VP016 for smokier jazz-soul timing.

### VP003 — Neon Falsetto Runner
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Airy neon tenor with falsetto flashes and agile syncopation.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** high tenor voice with airy neon timbre, breathy falsetto accents, clean light mix, quick register flips, minimal vibrato, and agile syncopated diction. Future-bass electropop arrangement with glossy sidechain pads and bright drums. Verse 1 should stay intimate and light, pre-chorus should lift through falsetto flicks, chorus should open with high floating vowels, bridge should soften into airy head voice, final chorus should add quick ad-lib flashes. Emphasize register flips, rhythmic agility, human breath. Avoid thin lifeless falsetto, robotic tuning, copied artist tone, and unstable pitch breaks.
- **Negative prompt / avoid list:** harsh autotune artifacts, squeaky falsetto, buried lead, random yelps.
- **Suggested Suno tags:** [Pulse Emerges], [Riser Build], [Big Chorus], [Ad-Lib Finale], [Afterglow]
- **Expected audible result:** A bright, nimble electronic lead whose identity is clearest in pre-chorus flips and final ad-libs.
- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP029 for processed alto coolness and VP044 for smoother modern tenor restraint.

### VP004 — Amber Belt Architect
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Amber mezzo mix-belt with controlled open-throat power.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** mezzo belter with amber bright-warm timbre, clear energized phonation, open-throat mix belt, crisp theatrical-pop diction, and delayed vibrato after straight attacks. Polished pop-rock arrangement with strong drums, piano/guitar support, and layered chorus vocals. Verse 1 should stay measured and low, pre-chorus should rise in clear steps, chorus should belt broad open vowels, bridge should pull back to soft mix, final chorus should return bigger with confident ad-libs. Emphasize controlled belt architecture and lyric clarity. Avoid constant shouting, excessive vibrato, and overcompressed vocals.
- **Negative prompt / avoid list:** shouting from first verse, strained highs, melodrama, muddy rock mix.
- **Suggested Suno tags:** [Rising Tension], [Big Chorus], [Return Bigger], [Ad-Lib Finale], [Resolved Cadence]
- **Expected audible result:** A strong radio-ready vocal arc with a satisfying belt payoff.
- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP010 for more theatrical diction and VP052 for symphonic high-register scale.

### VP005 — Chrome Melisma Weaver

Emphasize tasteful ornament and groove. Avoid over-singing every word, unintelligible runs, and excessive pitch correction.

- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP014 for devotional weight and VP001 for clean non-ornamented gloss.
