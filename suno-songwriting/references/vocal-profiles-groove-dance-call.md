# Groove and Dance-Call Vocal Profiles

VP031-VP036: clave, dembow, Afro-diasporic pocket, carnival scale, and call-response leadership.

#### VP031 — Dembow Silk Tenor
- **Human-readable summary:** A smooth adult tenor with silk tone, rap-sung agility, and dancehall/Latin-club rhythmic influence handled broadly and respectfully.
- **Voice coordinate vector:** vocal_role=tenor dance lead; approximate_range=A2–D5; tessitura=middle-high; vocal_weight=light-medium; timbre=silky, bright-warm; phonation=clear, lightly nasal; register_behavior=mix with airy highs; vibrato=minimal, pop-straight; ornamentation=slides, rhythmic repeats, small turns; articulation=percussive consonants, liquid vowels; diction/accent=broad Caribbean/Latin-club influenced vowel warmth, moderate foregrounding without parody; rhythmic_feel=dembow-adjacent syncopation, relaxed pocket; genre_gravity=reggaetón pop, Latin pop, dancehall-pop; emotional_stance=confident and warm; dynamic_shape=groove-first lift; production_texture=dembow entry, glossy percussion, warm bass; movement_behavior=verse rhythmic, chorus smooth, breakdown chant danceable.
- **Distinguishing features:** Silk tone over dembow-like groove and rhythmic diction.
- **Likely strengths:** Second verse, breakdown, dance choruses.
- **Likely weaknesses/failure modes:** Accent/diction can become caricature if over-specified; sustained chorus may flatten.
- **Best genres:** Latin club pop, reggaetón-pop, dancehall-pop, tropical electropop.
- **Contrasting nearest-neighbor profiles:** VP032 Clave Copper Alto; VP060 Carnival Circuit Lead.
- **Suno-relevant style vocabulary:** silky dance tenor, dembow groove, rap-sung phrasing, warm vowels, percussive diction.
- **What to listen for:** Whether rhythmic pocket remains strong while the lyric is clear.
