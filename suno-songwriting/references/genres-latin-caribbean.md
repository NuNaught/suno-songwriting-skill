# Latin, Caribbean, and Iberian Genres

Use for Spanish/Portuguese/Iberian/Caribbean/Latin American song, dance, and ensemble traditions.

| Genre name | Summary | Key identifying characteristics | Associated instruments |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Reggaetón                                  | Urban Caribbean-Latin popular genre with strong dance emphasis.                         | Dembow rhythm, repetitive hooks, rap-sung delivery, club-oriented production.                                        | Drum programming, synths, sampler, voice                                    |
| Dembow                                     | Dominican dance music centered on an aggressive looped rhythmic cell.                   | Sparse beat architecture, chant-like vocals, repetitive groove, raw party energy.                                    | Drum programming, sampler, synths, voice                                    |
| Cumbia villera                             | Gritty Argentine urban offshoot of cumbia.                                              | Simple dance grooves, colloquial lyrics, cheap-keyboard timbres, street realism.                                     | Keyboards, drum machine, electric bass, güiro                               |
| Son jarocho                                | Regional Mexican tradition from Veracruz with Afro-Indigenous-Iberian roots.            | Interlocking strummed rhythms, improvised verses, zapateado, communal fandango performance.                          | Jarana jarocha, requinto jarocho, harp, voice, foot percussion              |
| Samba-enredo                               | Large-ensemble samba created for Rio carnival parade schools.                           | Propulsive percussion battery, anthem-like refrains, massed ensemble drive, parade function.                         | Surdo, caixa, tamborim, cuíca, cavaquinho, voice                            |
| Frevo                                      | High-energy carnival music and dance from Pernambuco, Brazil.                           | Very fast tempo, brass fanfares, syncopated march energy, athletic dance style.                                      | Brass band, saxophones, percussion                                          |
| Tango nuevo                                | Modernized concert-oriented tango associated with expanded harmony and form.            | Sharp rhythmic cells, dramatic rubato, counterpoint, jazz/classical influence.                                       | Bandoneon, violin, piano, double bass, guitar                               |
| Fado                                       | Portuguese urban song tradition associated with saudade and intimate storytelling.      | Lyrical vocal expressivity, melancholic affect, rubato, text-centered delivery.                                      | Voice, Portuguese guitar, classical guitar                                  |
| Flamenco bulerías                          | Fast, festive flamenco palo from Andalusia.                                             | 12-beat cycle, handclaps, guitar rasgueado, melisma, dance interplay.                                                | Voice, flamenco guitar, palmas, cajón, dance                                |
| Son cubano                                 | Foundational Cuban son blending Spanish and Afro-Cuban elements.                        | Clave-based groove, montuno call-and-response, syncopated bass, song-to-jam structure.                               | Tres, bongos, maracas, claves, bass, trumpet, voice                         |
| Andean huayno                              | Highland Andean song-dance genre from Peru/Bolivia and nearby regions.                  | Pentatonic-inflected melodies, lively duple feel, alternating lyrical and dance energy.                              | Charango, quena, harp, violin, guitar, voice                                |
| Latin pop                                  | Mainstream Spanish-language pop with broad crossover appeal.                            | Strong hooks, polished vocal delivery, pop-ballad and dance-pop fusion, broad radio formatting.                      | Voice, guitar, keyboards, drum programming, bass                            |
| Bachata moderna                            | Modern mainstream Dominican bachata with pop production and crossover reach.            | Guitar arpeggios, romantic vocals, crisp percussion, smoother pop-oriented arrangements.                             | Requinto guitar, rhythm guitar, bass, bongos, güira, voice                  |
