# Pop, Rock, R&B, and Mainstream Genres

Use for modern commercial, band-based, rock, R&B, idol-pop, and radio-friendly style prompts.

| Genre name | Summary | Key identifying characteristics | Associated instruments |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Symphonic power metal                      | Melodic metal that combines power metal drive with orchestral scope.                    | High-register vocals, double-kick drumming, heroic themes, neoclassical harmony, symphonic layers.                   | Electric guitars, bass, drum kit, keyboards, orchestra/virtual orchestra    |
| Math rock                                  | Rock built from irregular meter and angular rhythmic design.                            | Odd meters, stop-start phrasing, tapped guitar lines, precision ensemble interplay.                                  | Electric guitars, bass, drum kit                                            |
| Post-rock                                  | Atmosphere-centered rock emphasizing texture over songcraft.                            | Long-form crescendos, delayed guitar timbres, sparse vocals or none, cinematic pacing.                               | Electric guitars, bass, drum kit, keyboards                                 |
| Contemporary pop                           | Broad commercial song form dominating global mainstream charts.                         | Hook-first writing, verse-pre-chorus-chorus structure, polished production, strong topline melodies.                 | Voice, synthesizers, drum programming, electric guitar, piano               |
| Pop rock                                   | Mainstream pop songwriting fused with rock band texture.                                | Catchy choruses, guitar-centered arrangements, steady backbeat, radio-friendly structure.                            | Voice, electric guitar, bass, drum kit, keyboards                           |
| Indie pop                                  | Melody-driven pop with lighter, more personal or textural production aesthetics.        | Jangly or soft synth textures, intimate vocals, concise songwriting, less aggressive mix profile.                    | Voice, guitar, bass, drum kit, synthesizers                                 |
| Synth-pop                                  | Pop built around synthesizer-centered arrangement and timbre.                           | Electronic textures, steady pulse, bright hooks, layered pads and arpeggiators.                                      | Synthesizers, drum machines, voice, sequencers                              |
| Electropop                                 | Contemporary pop with dance-oriented electronic production.                             | Glossy synth bass, punchy programmed drums, strong drops or chorus lifts, club-pop crossover.                        | Voice, synths, drum machines, samplers                                      |
| Dance-pop                                  | Pop specifically optimized for dancefloor-compatible mainstream appeal.                 | Four-on-the-floor or highly kinetic grooves, large choruses, polished vocal production.                              | Voice, synthesizers, drum programming, samplers                             |
| Contemporary R&B                           | Modern R&B blending soul vocals with hip-hop and electronic production.                 | Smooth melisma, syncopated vocal phrasing, atmospheric harmony, bass-heavy minimal beats.                            | Voice, drum programming, synths, electric piano, bass                       |
| Neo-soul                                   | Modern soul revival emphasizing groove, harmonic richness, and expressive phrasing.     | Extended chords, laid-back pocket, organic feel, jazz-influenced voicings.                                           | Voice, Rhodes, bass, drum kit, guitar, horns                                |
| Funk pop                                   | Mainstream pop shaped by funk rhythm and groove design.                                 | Syncopated bass, tight guitar rhythms, danceable pocket, bright chorus writing.                                      | Bass guitar, electric guitar, drum kit, keyboards, voice                    |
| Pop punk                                   | High-energy pop songwriting delivered through punk-derived band style.                  | Fast tempos, power chords, big singalong choruses, youthful emotional directness.                                    | Electric guitars, bass, drum kit, voice                                     |
| Alternative rock                           | Broad modern rock lane defined by non-classic-rock aesthetics but mass appeal.          | Guitar-led textures, dynamic contrast, introspective or edgy tone, flexible song structures.                         | Electric guitars, bass, drum kit, voice, keyboards                          |
| Metalcore                                  | Mainstream-adjacent heavy genre merging metal riffing with hardcore breakdowns.         | Alternation of screamed and clean vocals, breakdowns, downtuned guitars, polished aggression.                        | Electric guitars, bass, drum kit, voice                                     |
| K-pop                                      | Korean idol-centered commercial pop system spanning multiple production styles.         | Hybrid genre blending, sharp arrangement shifts, tightly designed hooks, choreographic orientation.                  | Voice, rap, synths, drum programming, samplers                              |
| J-pop                                      | Japanese mainstream pop with strong melody and polished commercial writing.             | Dense melodic content, bright harmonies, anime/idol crossover aesthetics, varied pop-rock-electronic fusion.         | Voice, guitar, bass, drum kit, synthesizers                                 |
| Latin pop                                  | Mainstream Spanish-language pop with broad crossover appeal.                            | Strong hooks, polished vocal delivery, pop-ballad and dance-pop fusion, broad radio formatting.                      | Voice, guitar, keyboards, drum programming, bass                            |
| Contemporary country pop                   | Modern country fused with polished mainstream pop production.                           | Verse-chorus pop forms, country lyric themes, glossy vocal production, restrained twang markers.                     | Voice, acoustic guitar, electric guitar, bass, drums, banjo                 |
| Country rock / bro-country mainstream lane | Arena-scale commercial country drawing heavily from rock and pop.                       | Big choruses, loud drums, distorted guitars, country signifiers inside pop-rock structure.                           | Voice, electric guitar, acoustic guitar, bass, drum kit                     |
| Singer-songwriter pop                      | Mainstream or near-mainstream pop centered on personal vocal storytelling.              | Lyric focus, relatively sparse accompaniment, strong melodic identity, intimate delivery.                            | Voice, piano, acoustic guitar, light rhythm section                         |
| Adult contemporary                         | Smooth, broadly accessible mainstream style aimed at wide radio listenership.           | Moderate tempos, polished arrangements, emotional clarity, low rhythmic aggression.                                  | Voice, piano, guitars, soft synths, drums                                   |
