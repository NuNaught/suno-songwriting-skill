# Jazz, Soul, and Gospel Vocal Prompts

VP013-VP018: timing nuance, vibrato control, ornament grammar, and ensemble-response behavior.

### VP013 — Blue Velvet Crooner
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Smooth blue-velvet baritone with relaxed swing.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** baritone crooner with blue-velvet timbre, warm slow vibrato, light breath, legato phrasing, and behind-the-beat swing. Jazz vocal/lounge-pop arrangement with warm piano, upright bass feel, brushed drums, and intimate room. Verse 1 should glide conversationally in low register, pre-chorus should lift elegantly, chorus should open with smooth sustained vowels, bridge should be very close and tender, breakdown should become a rhythmic spoken-sung swing figure, final chorus should add tasteful small ad-libs. Emphasize warmth and timing. Avoid sleepy lounge cliché, overlarge vibrato, and lyric smearing.
- **Negative prompt / avoid list:** parody lounge, drunk timing, buried consonants, excessive reverb.
- **Suggested Suno tags:** [Head In], [Locked Groove], [Big Chorus], [Trading Fours], [Afterglow]
- **Expected audible result:** A relaxed jazz-pop reading where timing creates the identity.
- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP018 for lighter tenor swing and VP022 for unpolished baritone storytelling.

### VP014 — Honeyed Gospel Anchor
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Honeyed gospel alto/mezzo with open-throat communal power.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** alto/mezzo gospel-soul voice with honeyed warm timbre, clear pressured phonation, open-throat lift, wide emotional vibrato, soulful slides, and natural call-and-response leadership. Gospel-soul/pop-soul arrangement with piano/organ warmth, handclaps, and group responses. Verse 1 should testify quietly, pre-chorus should rise with conviction, chorus should open into strong sustained vowels, bridge should be prayerful and exposed, breakdown should invite response, final chorus should become ecstatic with ad-libs and choir answers. Emphasize communal uplift. Avoid oversinging early, unintelligible runs, and exaggerated dialect.
- **Negative prompt / avoid list:** parody church accent, too many runs, shouty clipping, buried lead.
- **Suggested Suno tags:** [Call and Response], [Melismatic Lift], [Prayerful Peak], [Ecstatic Climax], [Ad-Lib Finale]
- **Expected audible result:** A powerful communal version with a clear emotional climb to the final chorus.
- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP005 for smoother R&B ornament and VP039 for spiral devotional repetition.

### VP015 — Pearl Scat Navigator
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Pearly agile jazz mezzo with precise improvised turns.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** jazz mezzo voice with pearly clean timbre, agile mix-head movement, quick delayed vibrato, crisp diction, scat-like turns, chromatic approach notes, and playful backphrasing. Chamber jazz/jazz-pop arrangement with live combo energy and harmonic color. Verse 1 should feel conversational and lightly swung, pre-chorus should rise through agile phrasing, chorus should stay direct but ornament phrase endings, bridge should become intimate and almost rubato, breakdown should be rhythmically playful, final chorus should add tasteful improvised fills. Emphasize precision and swing intelligence. Avoid cluttered scatting over lyrics, and losing the hook.
- **Negative prompt / avoid list:** nonsense scat over main lyrics, excessive chromatic runs, unclear words.
- **Suggested Suno tags:** [Head In], [Second Verse Switch-Up], [Trading Fours], [Lead Improvisation], [Resolved Cadence]
- **Expected audible result:** A nimble jazz-pop comparison where timing and ornaments are the key differentiators.
- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP018 for smoother swing and VP059 for non-improvised process precision.

### VP016 — Late-Night Cassia Alto
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Smoky cassia alto, languid and intimate.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** alto voice with smoky cassia timbre, breathy-clear phonation, mild rasp, slow narrow vibrato, soft legible consonants, and languid behind-the-beat phrasing. Jazz-soul/lounge R&B arrangement with close-mic warmth, electric piano, tape saturation, and smoky room ambience. Verse 1 should be almost spoken and low, pre-chorus should lean into warm slides, chorus should bloom without a hard belt, bridge should be fragile and exposed, breakdown should stay rhythmically clear, final chorus should add low smoky ad-libs. Emphasize late-night intimacy and world-weary tenderness. Avoid mumbled words, fake lounge parody, excessive rasp, and .
- **Negative prompt / avoid list:** overbreathy mumbling, cartoon smoky voice, sleepy tempo collapse.
- **Suggested Suno tags:** [Sparse Percussion], [Saudade Pause], [Big Chorus], [Quiet Contrast], [Afterglow]
- **Expected audible result:** A noir-tinted soul reading with smoky timbre and controlled softness.
- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP002 for cleaner alto confession and VP013 for baritone jazz croon.

### VP017 — Callhouse Lead
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Public call-and-response leader with percussive diction.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** call-and-response lead with ringing bright-warm timbre, projected clear phonation, percussive diction, brief energetic vibrato, and locked participatory groove. Gospel-funk/Afrobeat-soul arrangement with handclaps, group answers, bass groove, and warm room energy. Verse 1 should start as a direct address, pre-chorus should gather the ensemble, chorus should invite response lines, bridge should briefly strip to lead voice, breakdown should become a chanted hook, final chorus should be communal and ecstatic. Emphasize leadership, rhythm, and group interaction. Avoid solo diva excess, cluttered background vocals, and unclear responses.
- **Negative prompt / avoid list:** choir overpowering lead, messy call-response, exaggerated dialect, overbusy ad-libs.
- **Suggested Suno tags:** [Call and Response], [Groove Lock], [Chanted Hook], [Group Response], [Ecstatic Climax]
- **Expected audible result:** A participatory version where ensemble behavior is part of the vocal identity.
- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP014 for solo gospel weight and VP033 for Afro-pop bounce.

### VP018 — Satin Swing Tenor
- **Short voice identity phrase:** Light satin tenor crooner with romantic swing.
- **Full Suno style prompt:** tenor crooner with satin warm timbre, clean gentle phonation, light romantic vibrato, smooth legato, late scoops, and relaxed swing phrasing. Jazz-pop/lounge arrangement with warm brass/piano feel and tasteful rhythm section. Verse 1 should be low and charming, pre-chorus should rise elegantly, chorus should glide on open vowels instead of belting, bridge should soften into intimate near-whisper, breakdown should swing lightly, final chorus should add restrained romantic ad-libs. Emphasize elegance, phrasing, and vowel smoothness. Avoid syrupy parody, overlarge vibrato, and dull pacing.
- **Negative prompt / avoid list:** parody lounge, excessive croon wobble, muddy consonants, sleepy delivery.
- **Suggested Suno tags:** [Triple-Meter Lift], [Head In], [Big Chorus], [Lead Improvisation], [Afterglow]
- **Expected audible result:** A lighter, romantic swing interpretation of the same lyric.
- **Comparison notes:** Compare against VP013 for darker baritone croon and VP015 for more agile jazz phrasing.
