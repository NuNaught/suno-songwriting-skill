# Global Folk, Devotional, and Ritual Genres

Use for devotional, ritual, chamber, folk, regional vocal, and non-Western classical traditions.

| Genre name | Summary | Key identifying characteristics | Associated instruments |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Son jarocho                                | Regional Mexican tradition from Veracruz with Afro-Indigenous-Iberian roots.            | Interlocking strummed rhythms, improvised verses, zapateado, communal fandango performance.                          | Jarana jarocha, requinto jarocho, harp, voice, foot percussion              |
| Fado                                       | Portuguese urban song tradition associated with saudade and intimate storytelling.      | Lyrical vocal expressivity, melancholic affect, rubato, text-centered delivery.                                      | Voice, Portuguese guitar, classical guitar                                  |
| Flamenco bulerías                          | Fast, festive flamenco palo from Andalusia.                                             | 12-beat cycle, handclaps, guitar rasgueado, melisma, dance interplay.                                                | Voice, flamenco guitar, palmas, cajón, dance                                |
| Qawwali                                    | South Asian Sufi devotional performance tradition.                                      | Intensifying repetition, group refrains, handclaps, spiritual text emphasis, ecstatic climax.                        | Lead and chorus voices, harmonium, tabla, dholak, handclaps                 |
| Dhrupad                                    | Austere North Indian classical vocal tradition.                                         | Long unmetered alap, precise intonation, grave tone, rhythmic composition in fixed tala.                             | Voice, tanpura, pakhawaj, rudra veena                                       |
| Sankyoku                                   | Japanese chamber tradition centered on koto, shamisen, and shakuhachi.                  | Refined timbral balance, flexible ma, heterophonic interplay, courtly/chamber aesthetic.                             | Koto, shamisen, shakuhachi                                                  |
| Gagaku                                     | Ancient Japanese imperial court music.                                                  | Extremely slow pacing, ritual grandeur, heterophony, distinctive reed-and-wind sonorities.                           | Sho, hichiriki, ryuteki, biwa, koto, gakko percussion                       |
| Noh hayashi                                | Instrumental-vocal theater music of Japanese Noh drama.                                 | Stylized calls, sparse but pointed drum cues, ritualized pacing, dramatic restraint.                                 | Nohkan, ko-tsuzumi, otsuzumi, taiko, chant                                  |
| Gamelan gong kebyar                        | Explosive modern Balinese gamelan style.                                                | Sudden dynamic shifts, interlocking kotekan, shimmering tuned metallophones, virtuosic ensemble precision.           | Metallophones, gongs, kendang drums, suling                                 |
| Tuvan khoomei                              | Central Asian throat-singing tradition emphasizing overtone control.                    | Simultaneous pitch and overtone effect, drone emphasis, nature-evoking timbres.                                      | Voice, igil, doshpuluur                                                     |
| Andean huayno                              | Highland Andean song-dance genre from Peru/Bolivia and nearby regions.                  | Pentatonic-inflected melodies, lively duple feel, alternating lyrical and dance energy.                              | Charango, quena, harp, violin, guitar, voice                                |
| Hardanger fiddle tradition                 | Norwegian fiddle dance music with resonant drone-rich sound.                            | Sympathetic strings, asymmetrical dance rhythms, ornamented fiddle lines.                                            | Hardanger fiddle                                                            |
| Sevdalinka                                 | Bosnian urban song tradition noted for lyrical intimacy.                                | Ornamented melody, emotional rubato, poetic melancholy, flexible accompaniment.                                      | Voice, accordion, saz, violin                                               |
| Klezmer                                    | Ashkenazi Jewish instrumental wedding and celebration music.                            | Laughing/crying ornaments, modal inflection, dance sets, expressive lead lines.                                      | Clarinet, violin, tsimbl/cimbalom, accordion, brass                         |
| Bulgarian women’s choir folk style         | Balkan vocal tradition known for striking choral sonorities.                            | Open-throated timbre, close dissonances, asymmetric rhythms, powerful ensemble blend.                                | Women’s voices, occasional gaida or percussion                              |
