# Movement and Energy Tags

Energy, tension, release, tempo-feel, and pulse-shaping tags.

## Energy and motion
- [Slow Build] — Lets intensity rise patiently through layering, register, density, or harmonic pressure. Expected result: a larger payoff when the release finally arrives. Consider using in genres that reward suspense, atmosphere, or delayed gratification.

- [Rising Tension] — Signals that pressure is actively increasing toward a release point. Expected result: keeps the listener leaning forward and makes the next section feel earned. Consider using before a chorus, drop, climax, or rhythmic entry.

- [Release] — Marks the point where built-up tension resolves into groove, hook, catharsis, or openness. Expected result: emotional satisfaction and structural payoff. Consider using after buildup-heavy sections or dramatic withholding.

- [Final Surge] — A late-stage escalation that gives the ending extra force beyond the previous peak. Expected result: makes the track finish stronger than it started and avoids tapering too early. Consider using for final choruses, climaxes, codas, or last drops.

- [Afterglow] — A gentle, lingering comedown after a climax or high-intensity passage. Expected result: leaves emotional resonance instead of a purely functional finish. Consider using when you want the ending to feel reflective, euphoric, or suspended.

## Pulse and tempo behavior
- [Free Time] — Loosens or removes strict meter so phrasing follows breath, speech, or ritual motion. Expected result: heightened intimacy, gravity, or ancient/ceremonial feel. Consider using for invocations, dramatic openings, devotional material, or rubato traditions.

- [Rubato Entrance] — Begins with flexible timing before settling into pulse. Expected result: adds expressive human shape and makes the later groove feel more decisive. Consider using when you want an emotive opening that blooms into meter.

- [Pulse Emerges] — Introduces the beat gradually out of an ambient, free, or sparse passage. Expected result: gives the groove a sense of arrival rather than assumption. Consider using in cinematic builds, trance forms, and slow-burn arrangements.

- [Locked Groove] — Indicates a stable rhythmic pocket that should feel confident and sustained. Expected result: strong bodily engagement and dependable momentum. Consider using when danceability, pocket, or hypnotic repetition is central.

- [Half-Time Shift] — Reframes the same tempo grid to feel slower and heavier. Expected result: more weight, swagger, or dramatic impact without actually halving the BPM. Consider using for breakdowns, heavy drops, trap/metal pivots, or tension resets.

- [Double-Time Lift] — Reframes the pulse to feel faster and more urgent. Expected result: excitement, forward thrust, and increased kinetic energy. Consider using to heighten climaxes, intensify solos, or make a section feel like it’s catching fire.

- [Accelerando] — Gradually increases tempo over a passage. Expected result: rising urgency and physical escalation. Consider using in ritual build, orchestral momentum, folk-dance intensification, or dramatic final ascent.

- [Ritardando] — Gradually slows the tempo to widen emotional emphasis or closure. Expected result: gives the ending or transition more gravity and deliberateness. Consider using for cadences, dramatic stops, or reflective exits.
