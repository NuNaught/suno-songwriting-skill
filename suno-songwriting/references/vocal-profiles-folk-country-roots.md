# Folk, Country, and Roots Vocal Profiles

VP019-VP024: story-forward diction, narrower ranges, vernacular inflection, and acoustic plausibility.

#### VP019 — Cedar Thread Narrator
- **Human-readable summary:** A plainspoken adult folk alto with cedar-warm timbre, narrow range, and lyric-first storytelling.
- **Voice coordinate vector:** vocal_role=folk alto narrator; approximate_range=G3–B4; tessitura=middle-low; vocal_weight=light-medium; timbre=cedar, woody, dry-warm; phonation=clear, unadorned; register_behavior=chest-centered, little belt; vibrato=small natural wobble; ornamentation=almost none, slight scoops; articulation=natural consonants, conversational; diction/accent=broad rural-neutral hints, subtle foregrounding; rhythmic_feel=steady, speech-shaped; genre_gravity=folk, singer-songwriter, acoustic roots; emotional_stance=honest, reflective; dynamic_shape=small human swell; production_texture=acoustic close mic, room realism; movement_behavior=verse carries identity, chorus opens modestly, bridge intimate.
- **Distinguishing features:** Anti-polish sincerity and woody tone.
- **Likely strengths:** Lyric comprehension, verses, acoustic forms.
- **Likely weaknesses/failure modes:** May seem understated in big chorus; limited ornament variety.
- **Best genres:** Folk, acoustic singer-songwriter, indie roots.
- **Contrasting nearest-neighbor profiles:** VP020 High Lonesome Kite; VP043 North Sea Plainspoken Alto.
- **Suno-relevant style vocabulary:** cedar folk alto, plainspoken storytelling, dry close mic, minimal vibrato.
- **What to listen for:** Whether emotional specificity survives without vocal showmanship.

#### VP020 — High Lonesome Kite
- **Human-readable summary:** A high, piercing adult roots tenor/alto profile with narrow nasal brightness, straight-tone sustain, and mountain-air lift.
- **Voice coordinate vector:** vocal_role=high roots lead; approximate_range=B3–E5; tessitura=high; vocal_weight=light but piercing; timbre=bright, nasal-twang, clear; phonation=clear, forward; register_behavior=heady mix, open breaks; vibrato=mostly straight tone; ornamentation=slides, yodel-like breaks used sparingly; articulation=sharp consonants, open vowels; diction/accent=Appalachian-adjacent broad vowel color, moderate but respectful; rhythmic_feel=straight, slightly urgent; genre_gravity=bluegrass, old-time, folk-pop; emotional_stance=lonesome yearning; dynamic_shape=high chorus lift; production_texture=acoustic string-band compatible; movement_behavior=verse lean and high, chorus keening, final ad-libs with controlled breaks.
- **Distinguishing features:** Piercing high twang and straight-tone sustain.
- **Likely strengths:** Choruses, refrains, acoustic ensemble cut-through.
- **Likely weaknesses/failure modes:** Can become nasal caricature if overdone; low verse may be thin.
- **Best genres:** Bluegrass, folk, country-folk, acoustic pop.
- **Contrasting nearest-neighbor profiles:** VP019 Cedar Thread Narrator; VP024 Appalachian Bell Tenor.
- **Suno-relevant style vocabulary:** high lonesome tone, forward twang, straight-tone sustain, controlled yodel break.
- **What to listen for:** Whether twang remains musical and respectful rather than comic.

- **Likely strengths:** Story songs, choruses, emotionally direct lines.
- **Likely weaknesses/failure modes:** Accent can overtake lyric; vibrato may wobble too much.

- **Suno-relevant style vocabulary:** country mezzo, porchlight warmth, tremolo vibrato, relaxed vowels, gentle belt.

#### VP022 — Dustbowl Story Baritone
- **Human-readable summary:** A dry adult baritone storyteller with weathered diction, narrow melodic range, and moral gravity.
- **Voice coordinate vector:** vocal_role=baritone narrator; approximate_range=E2–B3; tessitura=low; vocal_weight=medium; timbre=dusty, dry, woody; phonation=speech-like clear; register_behavior=chest only; vibrato=rare, rough at endings; ornamentation=none except downward falls; articulation=plain, consonant-forward; diction/accent=roots storyteller, subtle-to-moderate foregrounding; rhythmic_feel=spoken-sung, slightly behind; genre_gravity=Americana, folk blues, roots ballad; emotional_stance=weathered witness; dynamic_shape=low steady arc; production_texture=dry acoustic, tape hiss optional; movement_behavior=spoken verse and breakdown excel, chorus stays low and resolute.
- **Distinguishing features:** Low narrative gravity over melodic display.
- **Likely strengths:** Lyric storytelling, rhythmic second verse, breakdown.
- **Likely weaknesses/failure modes:** May underdeliver open-vowel chorus; too much gravel can obscure words.
- **Best genres:** Americana, folk blues, spoken roots, noir country.
- **Contrasting nearest-neighbor profiles:** VP007 Gravel Lantern Tenor; VP013 Blue Velvet Crooner.
- **Suno-relevant style vocabulary:** dustbowl baritone, spoken-sung roots, dry diction, weathered witness.
- **What to listen for:** Whether the chorus feels like conviction rather than lack of range.

#### VP023 — Riverbend Yodel Alto
- **Human-readable summary:** A playful adult alto/mezzo with agile country-folk breaks, clean vowels, and riverbend melodic turns.
- **Voice coordinate vector:** vocal_role=alto/mezzo roots lead; approximate_range=G3–E5; tessitura=middle-high; vocal_weight=light-medium; timbre=bright-warm, fresh, slightly nasal; phonation=clear; register_behavior=audible chest-head breaks used ornamentally; vibrato=light and quick; ornamentation=yodel breaks, flips, country scoops; articulation=crisp, buoyant; diction/accent=country-folk vowel color, moderate but respectful; rhythmic_feel=light bounce; genre_gravity=country folk, bluegrass pop, western swing; emotional_stance=playful courage; dynamic_shape=quick bright lifts; production_texture=acoustic band with clean lead; movement_behavior=pre-chorus flips, chorus open yodel hints, final ad-libs playful.
- **Distinguishing features:** Controlled yodel breaks and buoyant diction.
- **Likely strengths:** Hooks, ad-libs, roots color, chorus lift.
- **Likely weaknesses/failure modes:** Breaks may become gimmicky; quiet bridge may lose seriousness.
- **Best genres:** Country-folk, bluegrass-pop, western swing, acoustic pop.
- **Contrasting nearest-neighbor profiles:** VP021 Porchlight Tremolo; VP020 High Lonesome Kite.
- **Suno-relevant style vocabulary:** riverbend alto, controlled yodel breaks, country scoops, buoyant roots vocal.
- **What to listen for:** Whether the yodel behavior appears sparingly and musically.

#### VP024 — Appalachian Bell Tenor
- **Human-readable summary:** A ringing adult tenor with clear bell-like projection, high roots diction, and communal singalong strength.
- **Voice coordinate vector:** vocal_role=roots tenor lead; approximate_range=A2–E4; tessitura=middle-high; vocal_weight=light-medium; timbre=bell-bright, forward, clean; phonation=clear, open; register_behavior=high chest/mix, minimal falsetto; vibrato=straight-to-slight; ornamentation=small slides, group-harmony-friendly endings; articulation=clear and ringing; diction/accent=Appalachian-adjacent vowel color, subtle-moderate; rhythmic_feel=steady and communal; genre_gravity=bluegrass, folk-rock, old-time pop; emotional_stance=earnest uplift; dynamic_shape=chorus singalong expansion; production_texture=acoustic strings and harmony stack; movement_behavior=chorus invites harmony, breakdown chant folk-like, outro resolves.
- **Distinguishing features:** Bell tone optimized for group harmony.
- **Likely strengths:** Singalong choruses, ensemble blends, acoustic clarity.
- **Likely weaknesses/failure modes:** Less distinctive in electronic textures; can become too straight emotionally.
- **Best genres:** Bluegrass, folk-rock, acoustic pop, roots gospel.
- **Contrasting nearest-neighbor profiles:** VP020 High Lonesome Kite; VP017 Callhouse Lead.
- **Suno-relevant style vocabulary:** ringing roots tenor, bell tone, straight-tone harmony, acoustic singalong.
- **What to listen for:** Whether harmony participation changes the final chorus scale.
