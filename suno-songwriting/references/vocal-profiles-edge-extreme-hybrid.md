# Edge and Extreme Hybrid Vocal Profiles

VP049-VP054: rasp, screams, doom, industrial tone, and high-force edge profiles.

#### VP049 — Razor Choir Screamer
- **Human-readable summary:** An extreme adult heavy-music profile alternating controlled harsh vocals with clean group-supported chorus moments.
- **Voice coordinate vector:** vocal_role=metalcore harsh/clean lead; approximate_range=G2–D5 apparent; tessitura=mid for harsh, upper-mid for clean; vocal_weight=heavy; timbre=razor, aggressive, bright distortion; phonation=controlled scream/growl plus clean contrast; register_behavior=harsh chest, clean mix chorus; vibrato=none in harsh, brief in clean; ornamentation=screamed attacks, gang shouts; articulation=percussive, consonant-heavy; diction/accent=neutral heavy diction, hidden; rhythmic_feel=half-time breakdown, urgent double-time accents; genre_gravity=metalcore, post-hardcore, heavy electronic rock; emotional_stance=rebellious catharsis; dynamic_shape=violent contrast; production_texture=downtuned guitars, breakdown, gang vocals; movement_behavior=verse harsh-spoken, chorus clean/huge, breakdown screamed chant, final chorus mixed.
- **Distinguishing features:** Harsh/clean contrast as formal identity.
- **Likely strengths:** Breakdown, high-contrast final chorus, aggressive reinterpretation.
- **Likely weaknesses/failure modes:** Harsh vocals may destroy lyric intelligibility; instability risk high.
- **Best genres:** Metalcore, post-hardcore, trap-metal hybrid, industrial rock.
- **Contrasting nearest-neighbor profiles:** VP051 Neon Punk Nasal Lead; VP052 Symphonic Steel Soprano.
- **Suno-relevant style vocabulary:** controlled harsh vocal, clean chorus contrast, half-time breakdown, gang shouts, avoid unintelligible screaming.
- **What to listen for:** Whether the common lyric remains identifiable in heavy sections.
