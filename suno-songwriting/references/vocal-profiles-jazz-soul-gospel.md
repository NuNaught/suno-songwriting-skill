# Jazz, Soul, and Gospel Vocal Profiles

VP013-VP018: timing nuance, vibrato control, ornament grammar, and ensemble-response behavior.

#### VP013 — Blue Velvet Crooner
- **Human-readable summary:** A smooth adult baritone crooner with blue-velvet tone, relaxed swing, and intimate long-phrase control.
- **Voice coordinate vector:** vocal_role=baritone crooner; approximate_range=F2–D4; tessitura=middle-low; vocal_weight=medium-light; timbre=blue velvet, dark smooth; phonation=clear, lightly breathy; register_behavior=chest-rich, gentle mix on peaks; vibrato=slow, warm, sustained; ornamentation=slides, jazz turns, soft falls; articulation=legato with clean consonant placement; diction/accent=neutral lounge diction, subtle foregrounding; rhythmic_feel=behind-the-beat swing; genre_gravity=jazz vocal, lounge, adult contemporary; emotional_stance=wry tenderness; dynamic_shape=small but elegant crescendos; production_texture=warm room, upright bass/piano-compatible; movement_behavior=verse relaxed, chorus opens elegantly, bridge quiet and close.
- **Distinguishing features:** Swing placement and velvet baritone warmth.
- **Likely strengths:** Low verse, rubato bridge, elegant endings.
- **Likely weaknesses/failure modes:** May underplay rhythmic breakdown; chorus may not feel climactic.
- **Best genres:** Jazz ballad, lounge pop, adult contemporary, soft swing.
- **Contrasting nearest-neighbor profiles:** VP018 Satin Swing Tenor; VP022 Dustbowl Story Baritone.
- **Suno-relevant style vocabulary:** velvet baritone crooner, behind-the-beat swing, warm vibrato, lounge intimacy.
- **What to listen for:** Whether swing feel changes the lyric without changing the words.

#### VP014 — Honeyed Gospel Anchor
- **Human-readable summary:** A full adult alto/mezzo with honeyed tone, devotional power, strong vibrato, and natural call-and-response leadership.
- **Voice coordinate vector:** vocal_role=alto/mezzo gospel lead; approximate_range=F3–E5; tessitura=middle; vocal_weight=medium-heavy; timbre=honeyed, warm, golden; phonation=clear with soulful pressure; register_behavior=chest-mix, open-throat lift; vibrato=wide, warm, emotional; ornamentation=melisma, shouts, slides, grace notes; articulation=clear but vowel-rich; diction/accent=church-informed studio diction, moderate foregrounding without caricature; rhythmic_feel=laid-back but driving; genre_gravity=gospel, soul, pop-soul; emotional_stance=devotional triumph; dynamic_shape=slow build to ecstatic peak; production_texture=organ/piano-compatible, group response, warm room; movement_behavior=pre-chorus lift, chorus testimony, final call-response.
- **Distinguishing features:** Devotional power and communal final-chorus behavior.
- **Likely strengths:** Final chorus, ad-libs, call-and-response, sustained vowels.
- **Likely weaknesses/failure modes:** Can over-sing early sections; ornament can obscure text.
- **Best genres:** Gospel-soul, pop-soul, worship-adjacent secular uplift, bluesy ballad.
- **Contrasting nearest-neighbor profiles:** VP005 Chrome Melisma Weaver; VP017 Callhouse Lead.
- **Suno-relevant style vocabulary:** honeyed gospel alto, open-throat lift, warm wide vibrato, call-and-response, devotional climax.
- **What to listen for:** Whether the final chorus feels communal while the lyric stays intelligible.

- **Voice coordinate vector:** vocal_role=jazz mezzo lead; approximate_range=A3–E5; tessitura=middle-high; vocal_weight=light; timbre=pearl, clean, agile; phonation=clear; register_behavior=mix-head agility; vibrato=quick controlled vibrato, often delayed; ornamentation=scat-like turns, approach-note slides, chromatic inflections; articulation=precise, buoyant consonants; diction/accent=clear jazz diction, subtle; rhythmic_feel=swung, syncopated, playful backphrasing; genre_gravity=bebop vocal, jazz-pop, third-stream pop; emotional_stance=clever, bright, resilient; dynamic_shape=nimble terraced phrases; production_texture=live jazz combo or chamber-jazz sheen; movement_behavior=verse conversational, pre-chorus agile, breakdown rhythmically playful.

- **Likely weaknesses/failure modes:** Can become too busy; open chorus may lose directness.

- **Contrasting nearest-neighbor profiles:** VP018 Satin Swing Tenor; VP059 Minimalist Pulse Alto.
- **Suno-relevant style vocabulary:** pearly jazz mezzo, agile scat turns, swung phrasing, crisp consonants, live combo feel.
- **What to listen for:** Whether rhythmic intelligence is audible while still serving the song.

#### VP016 — Late-Night Cassia Alto
- **Human-readable summary:** A smoky adult alto with late-night warmth, subtle rasp, and intimate jazz-soul phrasing.
- **Voice coordinate vector:** vocal_role=alto lead; approximate_range=F3–C5; tessitura=middle-low; vocal_weight=medium; timbre=smoky cassia, warm spice, dark; phonation=breathy-clear with mild rasp; register_behavior=chest-mix, soft high notes; vibrato=slow, narrow-to-medium; ornamentation=slides, sighing falls, small blues turns; articulation=soft but legible; diction/accent=urban lounge diction, subtle-moderate foregrounding; rhythmic_feel=behind-the-beat, languid; genre_gravity=jazz-soul, neo-soul, lounge R&B; emotional_stance=world-weary intimacy; dynamic_shape=low simmer to warm bloom; production_texture=close mic, tape warmth, smoky room; movement_behavior=verse nearly spoken, chorus warmed not belted, bridge exposed.
- **Distinguishing features:** Smoky alto intimacy with just enough rasp.
- **Likely strengths:** Low verse, quiet bridge, sensual phrasing.
- **Likely weaknesses/failure modes:** May blur rhythm in breakdown; choruses can become too subdued.
- **Best genres:** Jazz-soul, lounge R&B, noir pop, neo-soul.
- **Contrasting nearest-neighbor profiles:** VP002 Velvet Alto Courier; VP013 Blue Velvet Crooner.
- **Suno-relevant style vocabulary:** smoky alto, late-night close mic, behind-the-beat phrasing, warm tape room.
- **What to listen for:** Whether smoky tone remains intelligible in the rhythmic verse.

#### VP017 — Callhouse Lead
- **Human-readable summary:** A strong adult tenor/mezzo call leader built around communal response, rhythmic commands, and ecstatic repetition.
- **Voice coordinate vector:** vocal_role=call-and-response lead; approximate_range=A2–D5; tessitura=middle; vocal_weight=medium-heavy; timbre=bright-warm, public, ringing; phonation=clear and projected; register_behavior=chest-mix, shout-capable; vibrato=brief, energetic; ornamentation=shouted lifts, repeated hooks, response cues; articulation=percussive and clear; diction/accent=community-choir diction, moderate foregrounding; rhythmic_feel=locked groove, participatory; genre_gravity=gospel, Afrobeat, soul-funk, carnival pop; emotional_stance=ecstatic invitation; dynamic_shape=group build; production_texture=lead plus group response, room energy; movement_behavior=choruses invite answers, breakdown chant leads, final chorus communal.
- **Distinguishing features:** Ensemble-lead behavior rather than soloist display.
- **Likely strengths:** Breakdown, final chorus, crowd hooks.
- **Likely weaknesses/failure modes:** Solo bridge may feel less distinctive; group vocals can overpower lead.
- **Best genres:** Gospel-soul, Afrobeat-pop, funk, carnival-inspired pop.
- **Contrasting nearest-neighbor profiles:** VP014 Honeyed Gospel Anchor; VP033 Lagos Sun Callleader.
- **Suno-relevant style vocabulary:** call-and-response lead, communal chorus, percussive diction, ecstatic repetition, group vocals.
- **What to listen for:** Whether response voices feel structurally integrated rather than pasted on.

#### VP018 — Satin Swing Tenor
- **Human-readable summary:** A light adult tenor with satin tone, elegant swing, and romantic line-shaping.
- **Voice coordinate vector:** vocal_role=tenor crooner; approximate_range=A2–E4; tessitura=middle; vocal_weight=light-medium; timbre=satin, clear, warm; phonation=clean with gentle air; register_behavior=mix upper notes, chest base; vibrato=light, regular, romantic; ornamentation=slides, small mordents, late scoops; articulation=smooth legato with crisp phrase starts; diction/accent=standard lounge, subtle; rhythmic_feel=swinging, behind the beat; genre_gravity=jazz-pop, classic lounge, soft big-band pop; emotional_stance=playful devotion; dynamic_shape=gentle waves; production_texture=warm brass/piano-compatible vocal; movement_behavior=chorus glides rather than belts, bridge softens, final chorus adds tasteful ad-libs.
- **Distinguishing features:** Satin tenor warmth and relaxed swing.
- **Likely strengths:** Chorus phrasing, verse elegance, romantic delivery.
- **Likely weaknesses/failure modes:** May not handle aggressive breakdown; can become too polite.
- **Best genres:** Jazz-pop, lounge, swing ballad, adult contemporary.
- **Contrasting nearest-neighbor profiles:** VP013 Blue Velvet Crooner; VP015 Pearl Scat Navigator.
- **Suno-relevant style vocabulary:** satin tenor crooner, light swing, romantic vibrato, legato jazz phrasing.
- **What to listen for:** Whether the open-vowel chorus floats without losing emotional urgency.
