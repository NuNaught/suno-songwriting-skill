# African and Diasporic Groove Genres

Use for African, Maghrebi, Swahili coastal, diasporic, carnival, and groove-centered dance traditions.

| Genre name | Summary | Key identifying characteristics | Associated instruments |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Baile funk                                 | Brazilian favela-based electronic dance style.                                          | Heavy percussion loops, MC-driven flow, raw bass, call-and-response energy.                                          | Drum machines, samplers, voice                                              |
| Samba-enredo                               | Large-ensemble samba created for Rio carnival parade schools.                           | Propulsive percussion battery, anthem-like refrains, massed ensemble drive, parade function.                         | Surdo, caixa, tamborim, cuíca, cavaquinho, voice                            |
| Gnawa                                      | North African spiritual trance music with sub-Saharan roots.                            | Cyclical grooves, call-and-response, trance accumulation, metallic castanet pulse.                                   | Guembri, qraqeb, voice                                                      |
| Rai                                        | Algerian popular genre blending local styles with modern urban production.              | Ornamental vocals, Arabic/Maghrebi modes, pop hooks, danceable groove.                                               | Voice, keyboards, drum machine, oud, violin                                 |
| Mbaqanga                                   | South African township style mixing Zulu idioms and electric-band groove.               | Bouncy bass lines, guitar jive patterns, layered vocals, dance pulse.                                                | Electric guitar, bass, drum kit, keyboards, voice                           |
| Highlife                                   | West African dance-band tradition with guitar and horn-driven elegance.                 | Syncopated guitar lines, lilting horn riffs, urbane groove, melodic accessibility.                                   | Guitars, horns, percussion, bass, drum kit                                  |
| Kwaito                                     | South African post-apartheid urban dance music.                                         | Slower house-derived beats, chant-like vocals, deep grooves, local slang identity.                                   | Drum machines, synths, sampler, voice                                       |
| Afrobeat                                   | Large-ensemble West African groove music shaped by funk and political song.             | Extended vamps, polyrhythm, horn riffs, call-and-response, long-form build.                                          | Horn section, electric guitar, bass, keyboards, percussion, drum kit, voice |
| Taarab                                     | Swahili coastal art-pop/chamber tradition of East Africa.                               | Arabic-influenced melody, poetic texts, elegant ensemble texture, dance-song function.                               | Oud, qanun, violin, accordion, percussion, voice                            |
| Benga                                      | Kenyan guitar-led popular style with driving ostinati.                                  | Fast bass motion, interlocking guitar figures, dance pulse, bright ensemble texture.                                 | Electric guitars, bass, drums, vocals                                       |
| Afrobeats                                  | Contemporary West African and diasporic pop-dance sound distinct from classic Afrobeat. | Light syncopated grooves, melodic vocal lines, glossy production, rhythmic guitar or synth bounce.                   | Voice, drum programming, synths, electric guitar, bass                      |
| Amapiano                                   | South African dance genre that became a major global mainstream crossover.              | Deep log-drum bass, airy piano/synth layers, midtempo groove, spacious club feel.                                    | Synthesizers, drum programming, samplers, voice                             |
