# Rock, Theater, and Metal Force Vocal Profiles

VP007-VP012: projection, grit, distortion, belt, stage-scale dynamics, and heavy vocal force.

#### VP007 — Gravel Lantern Tenor
- **Human-readable summary:** A raspy adult tenor with warm grit, blues-rock phrasing, and a humane, lantern-like emotional glow.
- **Voice coordinate vector:** vocal_role=tenor rock lead; approximate_range=A2–D5; tessitura=middle-high; vocal_weight=medium; timbre=gravel, warm, smoky; phonation=controlled rasp over clear core; register_behavior=chest-mix with cracked edges; vibrato=irregular delayed vibrato; ornamentation=blue-note bends, scoops, growled phrase tails; articulation=rough consonants, vowel-stretched choruses; diction/accent=general roots-rock speech, subtle foregrounding; rhythmic_feel=behind-the-beat swing-rock; genre_gravity=blues rock, roots rock, soul-rock; emotional_stance=wounded resolve; dynamic_shape=grit increases toward final chorus; production_texture=tape-warm band room, mild saturation; movement_behavior=low verse smoke, chorus rasp bloom, breakdown half-spoken.
- **Distinguishing features:** Warm rasp rather than harsh scream.
- **Likely strengths:** Emotional wear, chorus grit, spoken-sung breakdown.
- **Likely weaknesses/failure modes:** Rasp may become uncontrolled distortion; high notes may sound strained.
- **Best genres:** Blues rock, roots rock, Americana, soul-rock.
- **Contrasting nearest-neighbor profiles:** VP053 Rusted Blues Shouter; VP022 Dustbowl Story Baritone.
- **Suno-relevant style vocabulary:** gravel tenor, controlled rasp, blue-note bends, tape-warm rock, wounded resolve.
- **What to listen for:** Whether grit follows the emotional arc instead of flattening the whole song.

#### VP008 — Iron Bloom Mezzo
- **Human-readable summary:** A powerful adult mezzo with dense tone, dramatic lift, and rock-ballad command; iron in the attack, bloom in the sustained vowels.
- **Voice coordinate vector:** vocal_role=mezzo rock lead; approximate_range=G3–E5; tessitura=middle-high; vocal_weight=heavy lyric; timbre=dark-bright, iron, resonant; phonation=clear with firm compression; register_behavior=chesty mix belt; vibrato=wide delayed vibrato on climaxes; ornamentation=few, mostly upward drive notes; articulation=bold, direct consonants; diction/accent=neutral rock, hidden; rhythmic_feel=straight, weighty; genre_gravity=alternative rock, power ballad, cinematic rock; emotional_stance=defiant grief; dynamic_shape=large terraced dynamics; production_texture=wide guitars or cinematic drums, strong vocal upfront; movement_behavior=pre-chorus pressure, big chorus, final surge.
- **Distinguishing features:** Heavy mezzo authority with controlled bloom.
- **Likely strengths:** Climactic choruses and bridge-to-final return.
- **Likely weaknesses/failure modes:** Can become too theatrical or too loud throughout.
- **Best genres:** Alternative rock, cinematic pop-rock, power ballad.
- **Contrasting nearest-neighbor profiles:** VP004 Amber Belt Architect; VP050 Velvet Doom Contralto.
- **Suno-relevant style vocabulary:** iron mezzo, heavy lyric belt, dramatic vibrato, cinematic rock, defiant chorus.
- **What to listen for:** Whether the quiet bridge truly contrasts the large chorus.

#### VP009 — Static Shout Baritone
- **Human-readable summary:** A dry, urgent adult baritone with punk-adjacent shouted singing, clipped consonants, and low melodic tolerance.
- **Voice coordinate vector:** vocal_role=baritone shouter; approximate_range=G2–C4; tessitura=middle-low; vocal_weight=medium-heavy; timbre=grainy, dry, nasal edge; phonation=speech-shout, lightly pressed; register_behavior=chest-only, minimal head voice; vibrato=none; ornamentation=none except fall-offs; articulation=clipped, consonant-heavy; diction/accent=urban conversational, moderate foregrounding; rhythmic_feel=ahead-of-beat, urgent; genre_gravity=post-punk, punk, garage rock; emotional_stance=rebel witness; dynamic_shape=flat high intensity with breakdown emphasis; production_texture=raw band, low polish, dry vocal; movement_behavior=spoken verse, shouted chorus, chant breakdown.
- **Distinguishing features:** Rhythmic urgency and anti-ornamental delivery.
- **Likely strengths:** Breakdown, rhythmic verse, direct lyric impact.
- **Likely weaknesses/failure modes:** Can fail sustained chorus notes; may sound monotone if melody not honored.
- **Best genres:** Punk, post-punk, garage rock, angular indie.
- **Contrasting nearest-neighbor profiles:** VP051 Neon Punk Nasal Lead; VP054 Blackroom Industrial Baritone.
- **Suno-relevant style vocabulary:** dry baritone shout, clipped consonants, no vibrato, raw post-punk, urgent speech-song.
- **What to listen for:** Whether the chorus maintains pitch enough while preserving shouted identity.

#### VP011 — Cathedral Edge Countertenor
- **Human-readable summary:** A high adult countertenor-like profile with cool head voice, choral purity, and a faint metallic edge for art-pop or sacred-hybrid settings.
- **Voice coordinate vector:** vocal_role=countertenor lead; approximate_range=G3–G5; tessitura=high; vocal_weight=light; timbre=cool, metallic, choral, pale; phonation=clean head-voice dominant; register_behavior=head voice sustained, rare chest anchoring; vibrato=straight tone with slight choral shimmer; ornamentation=minimal, stepwise turns; articulation=precise and legato; diction/accent=liturgical-neutral, subtle foregrounding; rhythmic_feel=floating, rubato-compatible; genre_gravity=classical crossover, art-pop, sacred electronic; emotional_stance=ceremonial, remote; dynamic_shape=controlled swell; production_texture=cathedral reverb or luminous chamber ambience; movement_behavior=pre-chorus ascends like invocation, chorus open but restrained, bridge ethereal.
- **Distinguishing features:** Head-voice purity and ceremonial distance.
- **Likely strengths:** Intro, bridge, choral layers, haunting chorus.
- **Likely weaknesses/failure modes:** May lack physical groove; consonants can blur in reverb.
- **Best genres:** Classical crossover, sacred art-pop, ambient chamber, neo-medieval pop.
- **Contrasting nearest-neighbor profiles:** VP037 Drone Lotus Baritone; VP040 Madrigal Silver Soprano.
- **Suno-relevant style vocabulary:** countertenor head voice, cathedral shimmer, straight tone, ceremonial art-pop, luminous reverb.
- **What to listen for:** Whether the voice feels human and focused inside spacious reverb.

#### VP012 — Smokehouse Roar
- **Human-readable summary:** A low-to-mid adult rock voice with smoky baritone color, roar-capable choruses, and restrained growl for dramatic peaks.
- **Voice coordinate vector:** vocal_role=baritone rock lead; approximate_range=F2–C4; tessitura=middle-low; vocal_weight=heavy; timbre=smoky, thick, charred; phonation=rasp and growl on emphasized words; register_behavior=chest-dominant, belt by force not height; vibrato=wide rough vibrato on held notes; ornamentation=growls, falls, bluesy bends; articulation=heavy consonants; diction/accent=roots-rock neutral with subtle foregrounding; rhythmic_feel=heavy backbeat, slightly behind; genre_gravity=hard rock, blues-rock, southern gothic rock; emotional_stance=stormy endurance; dynamic_shape=quiet smoke to roar; production_texture=thick guitars, warm distortion, room drums; movement_behavior=verse smolders, chorus roars, breakdown chant low.
- **Distinguishing features:** Low roar and thick smoke tone.
- **Likely strengths:** Rock arrangements, final chorus, breakdown authority.
- **Likely weaknesses/failure modes:** Can muddy lyrics; high chorus may strain.
- **Best genres:** Hard rock, blues-rock, gothic Americana, cinematic trailer-rock.
- **Contrasting nearest-neighbor profiles:** VP007 Gravel Lantern Tenor; VP054 Blackroom Industrial Baritone.
- **Suno-relevant style vocabulary:** smoky baritone roar, controlled growl, thick guitars, low chest power, stormy rock.
- **What to listen for:** Whether low power communicates without losing the melody.
