# Vocal Comparison Song

Shared original song, movement hints, and section requirements for comparing vocal profiles against identical lyrics.

## V. Common comparison song

### Song title
**Lantern in the Water**

### Overall song concept
A narrator stands at the edge of a dark crossing and decides to carry a small light forward. The lyric avoids highly specific instrumentation, era, or setting so it can flex across pop, rock, jazz, folk, R&B, gospel, electronic, theater, acoustic, devotional, dance, and experimental arrangements. The emotional stance can become confession, prayer, protest, romantic plea, survival anthem, ghost story, or communal chant depending on the vocal profile.

### Section-by-section structure

| Section | Function | Approximate duration |
|---|---|---:|
| Intro | Establish tone; optional rubato or motif | 0:00–0:12 |
| Verse 1 | Intimate low-register storytelling | 0:12–0:38 |
| Pre-Chorus | Rising intensity and register lift | 0:38–0:55 |
| Chorus | Open-vowel sustained payoff | 0:55–1:18 |
| Verse 2 | Rhythmic/spoken-sung diction test | 1:18–1:43 |
| Pre-Chorus 2 | Increased lift, room for melisma | 1:43–2:00 |
| Chorus 2 | Stronger delivery | 2:00–2:22 |
| Bridge | Quiet exposed contrast | 2:22–2:40 |
| Breakdown | Pulse/chant/spoken-sung reset | 2:40–2:55 |
| Final Chorus | Climactic return with ad-libs or call-response | 2:55–3:18 if allowed; compress to 3:00 by shortening repeats |
| Outro | Resolving phrase or afterglow | final 5–10 seconds |

For a strict 3:00 generation, use one final chorus pass plus a short outro. For a 3:15 version, repeat the final hook once.

### Vocal evaluation notes for each section

| Section | What to evaluate |
|---|---|
| Intro | Rubato control, onset quality, breath/noise floor, vowel color on “water” and “name.” |
| Verse 1 | Low-register comfort, lyric intelligibility, conversational phrasing, emotional microdynamics. |
| Pre-Chorus | Register transition, tension management, breath control, intensity without premature climax. |
| Chorus | Sustained open vowels, vibrato behavior, belt/head/mix choices, projection style. |
| Verse 2 | Rhythmic diction, consonant precision, spoken-sung ability, groove placement. |
| Pre-Chorus 2 | Ability to repeat material with more lift; tasteful melisma or ornament opportunity. |
| Chorus 2 | Consistency under increased energy; whether identity persists when arrangement grows. |
| Bridge | Exposed quiet singing, fragility, straight-tone/vibrato choices, emotional sincerity. |
| Breakdown | Chant, pulse, speech-song, callout strength, rhythmic stability. |
| Final Chorus | Ad-libs, call-response behavior, climactic identity, stability at peak. |
| Outro | Resolution, decrescendo, final vowel color, whether character remains after climax. |

### Movement hints for prompt injection

- Intro: rubato entrance or motif statement.
- Verse 1: intimate low-register delivery.
- Pre-chorus: rising tension and register lift.
- Chorus: open-vowel payoff, sustained notes, broad emotional release.
- Verse 2: rhythmic diction or spoken-sung treatment.
- Pre-chorus 2: increased lift with optional ornament.
- Bridge: exposed quiet contrast.
- Breakdown: pulse-based chant or spoken-sung hook.
- Final chorus: return bigger with ad-libs, group response, harmony stack, or climactic peak.
- Outro: afterglow, resolved cadence, ritual close, cold ending, or fade depending on profile.
